
# Meghalaya travel guide

**Discover the Hidden Gem of Meghalaya: A Travel Guide**

Tucked away in the northeastern corner of India, Meghalaya is a breathtaking haven that beckons adventure-seekers and nature lovers alike. This pristine state is a treasure trove of waterfalls, lush forests, rolling hills, and majestic mountain ranges. As you embark on this journey, you'll unravel the unspoiled beauty of Meghalaya, steeped in rich cultural heritage and warm hospitality. Get ready to immerse yourself in the untamed magnificence of Meghalaya.

**Best Time to Visit**

The perfect time to visit Meghalaya is during the spring months (March to May) when the climate is pleasant, averaging an ideal temperature of 18°C to 25°C. The misty morning and warm afternoons make it an ideal time for hiking, trekking, and exploring the outdoors. The monsoon months (June to September) can be quite wet, but the verdant landscape and cascading waterfalls create a breathtaking sight. Winter (October to February) is cooler, and the scenery is picturesque, with the hills blanketed in a blanket of greenery.

**How to Reach**

Meghalaya is relatively inaccessible due to limited connectivity and infrastructure development. You can reach the state by air, road, or rail. The nearest airport is Loktak Airport in Imphal, Manipur, but it's often subject to flight cancellations. A better option is to fly to Guwahati International Airport in Assam, which is the most accessible airport for Meghalaya. From Guwahati, you can rent a car or hire a taxi, which will take approximately 4 to 5 hours to reach Meghalaya. By rail, you can take the New Bongaigaon- Guwahati section of the Indian Railways, and then take a taxi or bus to reach Meghalaya.

**Top Places to Visit**

Meghalaya is a treasure trove of unexplored destinations, hidden gems, and breathtaking landscapes. Here are some must-visit places:

1. **Cherrapunji**: Known for being the wettest place on earth, Cherrapunji is home to the breathtaking Nohkalikai Falls, Mawsun Rock (the 4th highest in the world), and the Seven Sisters' Falls.
2. **Shillong**: The 'Scotland of the East' is a colonial town with British-era architecture, beautiful churches, and lush forests.
3. **Eco Park**: Located in the heart of Shillong, Eco Park is a natural haven with lush green forests, sparkling streams, and breathtaking views of the mountains.
4. **Mawsmai Cave**: Explore the unique rock formations and stunning natural beauty within the Mawsmai Cave, a limestone cave located near Cherrapunji.
5. **Laitlum Canyons**: The Laitlum Canyons offer breathtaking views of the rolling hills, valleys, and sparkling waterfalls, making it a must-visit location for any adventure-seeker.
6. **Mawlynnong**: This sleepy village is home to one of the cleanest villages in Asia, where traditional huts blend seamlessly with modern architecture.

**Travel Tips**

1. **Weather**: Check the weather forecast before traveling to Meghalaya. The weather can change rapidly.
2. **Infrastructure**: Meghalaya has limited connectivity and infrastructure. Be prepared for delays and rough roads.
3. **Food**: Try the local cuisine, which is rich in spices and has a unique flavor.
4. **Accommodation**: Stay in local hotels, guesthouses, or homestays for an immersive experience.
5. **Language**: Khasi and Pnar are the primary languages spoken in Meghalaya. However, Hindi and English are widely understood.
6. **Health**: Consult a doctor before traveling to Meghalaya due to its high altitude and change in climate.

**Budget Tips**

1. **Accommodation**: Stay in budget-friendly homestays and guesthouses.
2. **Transport**: Hire a local taxi or rent a car for better rates.
3. **Food**: Eat at local eateries or street food stalls for affordable options.
4. **Entrance fees**: Pay the entrance fees at the various attractions.
5. **Souvenirs**: Buy local handicrafts and souvenirs to support the local economy.

**Conclusion**

Meghalaya is a paradise for adventure-seekers, nature lovers, and wanderlusters alike. With its stunning landscapes, breathtaking waterfalls, and untouched natural beauty, this little-known gem is waiting to be explored. Plan your trip to Meghalaya and discover the magic of this enchanted land for yourself.

**Frequently Asked Questions**

Q: Is Meghalaya safe to visit?
A: Yes, Meghalaya is generally a safe destination, but take necessary precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.

Q: Can I get cash withdrawal facilities in Meghalaya?
A: Yes, cash withdrawal facilities are available in major towns, but ATMs may be limited in rural areas.

Q: What is the best time to visit Meghalaya?
A: The best time to visit Meghalaya is during the spring months (March to May) or the winter months (October to February).

Q: Can I hike in Meghalaya?
A: Yes, hiking and trekking are popular activities in Meghalaya, with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Q: What is the local cuisine like?
A: The local cuisine is rich in spices and has a unique flavor. Try the local specialties like bamboo shoot curry and momos.

Q: Can I communicate in English in Meghalaya?
A: Yes, English is widely understood in Meghalaya, making communication easy for tourists.
