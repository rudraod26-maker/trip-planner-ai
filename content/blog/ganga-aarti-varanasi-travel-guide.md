
# Ganga Aarti Varanasi travel guide

**Ganga Aarti Varanasi: A Spiritual Journey Along the Sacred Ganges**

Welcome to the city of Varanasi, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, steeped in history, spirituality, and mysticism. Located in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, Varanasi is a sacred destination for Hindus, attracting millions of pilgrims every year. Among the many experiences that Varanasi has to offer, the Ganga Aarti is a must-watch ceremony that showcases the city's spiritual essence. In this travel guide, we will walk you through the best time to visit, how to reach, top places to visit, day-wise itinerary, travel tips, and budget tips to make the most of your spiritual journey to Varanasi.

**Introduction**

Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Benares, is one of the seven sacred cities in Hinduism. The city's spiritual vibe is palpable in the narrow alleys, ancient temples, and sacred ghats that line the Ganges River. Among the many experiences that Varanasi has to offer, the Ganga Aarti is a once-in-a-lifetime experience that will leave you mesmerized. The Ganga Aarti is a ceremony performed every evening at sunset, where devotees offer their prayers and aaratis (prayers with lights) to the sacred Ganges River. In this blog, we will explore the best time to visit, how to reach, top places to visit, day-wise itinerary, travel tips, and budget tips to make your trip to Varanasi memorable.

**Best Time to Visit**

The best time to visit Varanasi is during the winter months from October to March. The weather is mild and pleasant, making it ideal for exploring the city. The temperature ranges from 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F), which is perfect for sightseeing. The Ganga Aarti ceremony is performed every day, but the best time to watch it is during the full moon night, known as Purnima. The ceremony is more vibrant and colorful during this time, attracting larger crowds.

**How to Reach**

Varanasi is well-connected by air, rail, and road to major cities in India. Here are the different ways to reach Varanasi:

* **By Air**: The Varanasi airport, Lal Bahadur Shastri Airport, is located about 18 kilometers from the city center. Flights are available from major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata. From the airport, you can take a taxi or auto-rickshaw to reach your hotel.
* **By Rail**: Varanasi has a well-connected railway network, with trains running from major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata. The Varanasi Junction railway station is the main railway station, located in the heart of the city.
* **By Road**: Varanasi is connected by bus to major cities in India. The bus stand is located near the Varanasi Station, from where you can take a taxi or auto-rickshaw to reach your hotel.

**Top Places to Visit**

Varanasi is a city that is steeped in history, spirituality, and mysticism. Here are the top places to visit in Varanasi:

* **Assi Ghat**: This is the most popular ghat in Varanasi, known for its lively atmosphere and stunning sunsets.
* **Dashaswamedh Ghat**: This is the oldest and most sacred ghat in Varanasi, where the Ganga Aarti ceremony is performed every day.
* **Manikarnika Ghat**: This ghat is one of the most sacred sites in Varanasi, where holy men and women go to burn their dead.
* **Kashi Vishwanath Temple**: This is one of the most sacred temples in Varanasi, dedicated to Lord Shiva.
* **Bharat Mata Mandir**: This temple is a shrine dedicated to Mother India, with a stunning view of the city.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Here is a day-wise itinerary to help you explore the best of Varanasi:

Day 1: Arrival and Ganga Aarti

* Arrive in Varanasi and check into your hotel
* Visit the Kashi Vishwanath Temple and offer your prayers
* Watch the Ganga Aarti ceremony at Dashaswamedh Ghat
* Explore the narrow alleys of the old city and try some local street food

Day 2: Ghats and Temples

* Visit the Assi Ghat and take a boat ride on the Ganges River
* Explore the Manikarnika Ghat and learn about the holy rituals performed here
* Visit the Bharat Mata Mandir and take in the stunning views of the city
* Explore the local markets and try some local handicrafts

Day 3: Spiritual Encounters

* Visit the Sarnath, the site where Lord Buddha first preached
* Meet with local priests and learn about the spiritual practices of Varanasi
* Explore the narrow alleys of the old city and interact with locals
* Attend a Satsang (spiritual gathering) in one of the many temples

**Travel Tips**

Here are some travel tips to make your trip to Varanasi memorable:

* **Respect local customs**: Varanasi is a sacred city, so respect local customs and traditions.
* **Dress modestly**: Dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing.
* **Be patient**: Varanasi is a city that is steeped in history and spirituality, so be patient and take your time.
* **Learn some Hindi**: Learning some basic Hindi phrases will help you interact with locals and enhance your experience.

**Budget Tips**

Here are some budget tips to help you plan your trip to Varanasi:

* **Accommodation**: Choose budget-friendly accommodation options like guesthouses or hostels.
* **Food**: Eat at local eateries or try some street food to save money.
* **Transportation**: Use local transportation like auto-rickshaws or buses to save money.
* **Activities**: Visit free or low-cost attractions like ghats, temples, and Satsangs.
