
# Bikaner travel guide

**Bikaner Travel Guide: Discover the Charm of This Royal Indian City**

Located in the northwestern state of Rajasthan, India, Bikaner is a city steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. This royal city is often overlooked by travelers flocking to more popular destinations in Rajasthan, but those who venture here are rewarded with a treasure trove of experiences that will leave them enchanted. In this comprehensive travel guide, we'll delve into the best time to visit Bikaner, how to reach this magnificent city, top places to visit, and a day-wise itinerary to help you make the most of your trip.

**Introduction**

Bikaner, with its rich history dating back to the 16th century, was founded by Rao Bikaji, a Rajput king, who named the city after himself. The city's strategic location made it a vital trade hub between the merchants of Europe, the Middle East, and Asia. Over the centuries, Bikaner has been influenced by various cultures, leaving it with a unique blend of architecture, art, and cuisine that reflects its rich heritage.

**Best Time to Visit**

The best time to visit Bikaner is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and cool, ranging from 15°C to 25°C. The summer months are unbearably hot, while the monsoon season is short and intense. If you're looking to witness the city's vibrant festivals, October is the best time, with the famous Bikaner Camel Festival taking place in November.

**How to Reach**

Bikaner is well-connected by air, rail, and road to major Indian cities.

* **By Air:** The nearest airport is Jodhpur Airport (JDH), which is 260 km away from Bikaner. From Jodhpur, you can take a taxi or bus to Bikaner.
* **By Rail:** The Bikaner Railway Station (BKN) is a major railway junction, with regular train services to Delhi, Jaipur, Jodhpur, and other major cities in India.
* **By Road:** Bikaner is connected to major cities in Rajasthan and India by a network of national highways.

**Top Places to Visit**

Bikaner is a treasure trove of historical and cultural attractions that will keep you engaged for days.

1. **Junagarh Fort**: This majestic fort, situated on a hill, was built in 1586 by Maharaja Raja Rai Singh, a general of Akbar's army. The fort has been beautifully restored and now houses a museum showcasing artifacts from the Rajput and Mughal periods.
2. **Lallgarh Palace**: This stunning palace, built in 1902, is a testament to the city's rich architectural heritage. The palace now houses the National Research Centre on Camel.
3. **Bikaner Camel Research Centre**: Located within the Lallgarh Palace complex, this center is dedicated to the breeding, conservation, and research of camels.
4. **Ganga Mandir**: This ancient temple, built in 1841, is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple is famous for its intricate carvings and stunning architecture.
5. **Karni Mata Temple**: This ancient temple, built in 1541, is dedicated to the legendary mystic, Karni Mata. The temple is famous for its large number of black rats that roam the premises.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

To make the most of your trip to Bikaner, here's a suggested day-wise itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Bikaner and explore the Lallgarh Palace, Bikaner Camel Research Centre, and Ganga Mandir.

Day 2: Visit the Junagarh Fort and explore the city's charming bazaars.

Day 3: Visit the Karni Mata Temple and explore the beautiful desert landscapes surrounding the city.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the nearby village of Gajner and enjoy the stunning views of the Aravalli Range.

**Travel Tips**

* **Language**: The official language of Bikaner is Rajasthani, but Hindi and English are widely spoken.
* **Cuisine**: Bikaner is famous for its delicious street food, including the famous "Bikaneri Pyaaj ka Kachori."
* **Weather**: The weather in Bikaner can be very hot during the summer months, so make sure to carry sunscreen, a hat, and plenty of water.
* **Safety**: The city is generally safe, but it's always a good idea to take the usual precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.

**Budget Tips**

Bikaner is a relatively affordable destination, with accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some budget tips to help you save money:

* **Accommodation**: Opt for budget-friendly guesthouses or hostels, which can cost between Rs 500 to Rs 1,500 per night.
* **Food**: Eat at local street food stalls or roadside dhabas, which offer delicious and affordable meals starting from Rs 50.
* **Transportation**: Use a taxi or rickshaw to get around the city, which costs around Rs 100 to Rs 200 per day.
* **Attractions**: Visit the Junagarh Fort and Lallgarh Palace in the early morning or late evening to avoid the peak tourist season.

**Conclusion**

Bikaner, with its rich history, stunning architecture, and vibrant culture, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. From the majestic Junagarh Fort to the charming Ganga Mandir, this city has something for everyone. Whether you're a history buff, an architecture enthusiast, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Bikaner is an ideal destination to add to your Indian travel itinerary.

In conclusion, Bikaner is a city that will leave you enchanted and inspired, with its unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Plan your trip now and experience the charm of this royal Indian city for yourself.
