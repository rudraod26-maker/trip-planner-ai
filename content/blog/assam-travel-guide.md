
# Assam travel guide

**Discover the Hidden Gem of Assam: A Travel Guide**

Located in the northeastern part of India, Assam is a treasure trove of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a plethora of exciting tourist attractions. From the majestic Brahmaputra River to the majestic Elephant Falls, Assam offers an unforgettable travel experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the best time to visit Assam, how to reach the state, must-visit places, a day-wise itinerary, travel tips, and budget tips to make your trip to Assam a memorable one.

**Best Time to Visit Assam**

The best time to visit Assam is from October to April, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for sightseeing. The temperature ranges from 15°C to 25°C during this period, making it ideal for exploring the state's many attractions. The summer months (June to September) can be quite hot, while the monsoon season (May to September) can be wet and humid. If you're planning to visit the tea gardens or the Kaziranga National Park, consider visiting during the winter months (October to April).

**How to Reach Assam**

Assam is well-connected to the rest of the country by air, rail, and road. The state capital, Guwahati, is the main hub for air and rail travel.

* **By Air:** Guwahati is connected to most major cities in India, including Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata. You can fly to Guwahati International Airport (GAU) and then connect to other parts of the state by road or rail.
* **By Rail:** Guwahati has a well-connected railway network with most major cities in India. You can take a train to Guwahati and then connect to other parts of the state by road or by taking a local train.
* **By Road:** Assam is connected to the neighboring states of Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, and Nagaland by road. You can take a bus or hire a car to travel to Assam.

**Top Places to Visit in Assam**

Assam is a treasure trove of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a plethora of exciting tourist attractions. Some of the top places to visit in Assam include:

* **Kaziranga National Park:** A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Kaziranga National Park is famous for its one-horned rhinos, tigers, and other wildlife species. Take a jeep safari or an elephant ride to explore the park.
* **Majuli Island:** The largest river island in the world, Majuli Island is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Take a ferry to the island and explore its scenic beauty, rich cultural heritage, and traditional satras.
* **Manas National Park:** A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Manas National Park is famous for its diverse wildlife and bird species. Take a trek or a jeep safari to explore the park.
* **Kamakhya Temple:** A sacred temple dedicated to the goddess Kamakhya, Kamakhya Temple is a must-visit for those interested in spirituality and culture.
* **Brahmaputra River:** Take a boat ride or a ferry to explore the majestic Brahmaputra River. You can also take a boat ride to the nearby islands for a scenic view.
* **Sivasagar:** A historic town known for its rich cultural heritage, Sivasagar is home to many ancient temples, monuments, and museums. Take a walk through the town to explore its scenic beauty and rich history.
* **Jorhat:** The tea capital of Assam, Jorhat is a charming town surrounded by lush tea gardens. Take a walk through the tea gardens or visit the nearby Kaziranga National Park for a scenic view.
* **Dibrugarh:** A charming town surrounded by lush tea gardens, Dibrugarh is a must-visit for those interested in tea and coffee.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Here's a suggested day-wise itinerary for your trip to Assam:

Day 1: Arrival in Guwahati

* Arrive at Guwahati International Airport (GAU)
* Check into your hotel and take a rest
* Visit the nearby Kamakhya Temple for a spiritual experience
* Take a boat ride on the Brahmaputra River for a scenic view

Day 2: Guwahati

* Visit the Guwahati Museum to explore the state's rich history and culture
* Take a walk through the nearby Umananda Island for a scenic view
* Visit the Assam State Museum to explore the state's art, culture, and history

Day 3: Kaziranga National Park

* Take a jeep safari or an elephant ride to explore the Kaziranga National Park
* Visit the Panidhuba Beel for a scenic view and birdwatching
* Take a walk through the park's nearby villages to explore the local culture and way of life

Day 4: Majuli Island

* Take a ferry to Majuli Island and explore its scenic beauty and rich cultural heritage
* Visit the nearby satras to learn about traditional Assamese culture and art

Day 5: Sivasagar and Jorhat

* Visit the Sivasagar district and explore its ancient temples, monuments, and museums
* Take a walk through the nearby tea gardens to explore the scenic beauty and rich history
* Visit the Jorhat market to buy local handicrafts and souvenirs

**Travel Tips**

Assam is a beautiful state with a rich cultural heritage and a plethora of exciting tourist attractions. Here are some travel tips to help you make the most of your trip:

* **Language:** Assamese and Bengali are the official languages of Assam. English is widely spoken in tourist areas, but it's always a good idea to learn a few basic phrases in the local language.
* **Currency:** Indian Rupee (INR) is the official currency of Assam. You can exchange your money at the airport or any local currency exchange office.
* **Weather:** The best time to visit Assam is from October to April, when the weather is pleasant and suitable for sightseeing.
* **Clothing:** Pack light and comfortable clothing for your trip to Assam. It's always a good idea to wear layers, as the temperature can drop significantly at night.
* **Accommodation:** Guwahati has a range of hotels and resorts to suit all budgets. You can also stay in local homestays or guesthouses for a more authentic experience.
* **Transportation:** Guwahati is well-connected to the rest of the state by air, rail, and road. You can hire a car or take a bus to travel to other parts of the state.

**Budget Tips**

Assam is a budget-friendly destination, but it's always a good idea to plan ahead and budget accordingly. Here are some budget tips to help you make the most of your trip:

* **Accommodation:** Budget hotels and local homestays are available in Guwahati and other parts of the state. You can also stay in government-run guesthouses or hostels for a more affordable option.
* **Food:** Local street food and restaurants are available in Guwahati and other parts of the state. Try the local delicacies like jadoi ( Assamese rice pudding), aloo pitika ( spicy potato curry), and masoor tenga ( lentil curry).
* **Transportation:** Hire a car or take a bus to travel to other parts of the state. You can also hire a bike or a motorcycle for a more affordable option.
* **Activities:** Kaziranga National Park and Majuli Island offer affordable packages for tourists, including guided tours and entrance fees.
* **Shopping:** Local markets and bazaars are available in Guwahati and other parts of the state. Try the local handicrafts and souvenirs, like traditional Assamese masks, pottery, and textiles.
