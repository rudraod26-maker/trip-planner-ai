
# Backwaters of Kerala travel guide

**Discover the Serenity of Kerala's Backwaters: A Travel Guide**

Kerala, a southern Indian state, is famous for its tranquil backwaters, which stretch along the coast of Alleppey and surrounding areas. The serene lakes, canals, and rivers are home to an array of wildlife, including dolphins, birds, and numerous water plants. If you're planning a trip to Kerala, exploring the backwaters is an absolute must. In this comprehensive travel guide, we'll take you through the best time to visit, how to reach, top places to visit, day-wise itinerary, travel tips, budget tips, and more.

**Introduction**

The Kerala backwaters are a network of interconnected rivers, canals, and lakes that make up a vast watery territory. This unique ecosystem has become a popular destination for tourists seeking relaxation, nature, and adventure. The backwaters offer a glimpse into rural Kerala life, showcasing the traditional villages, fishing communities, and their way of living. With its picturesque landscapes, lush vegetation, and tranquil atmosphere, exploring Kerala's backwaters is an unforgettable experience.

**Best Time to Visit**

The best time to visit Kerala's backwaters is from September to March, when the weather is mild and pleasant. This period offers ideal conditions for boat cruises, birdwatching, and spotting wildlife. Avoid visiting during the monsoon season (June to August) or the peak summer months (April to May), as the backwaters can get flooded or extremely hot.

**How to Reach**

Reaching Kerala's backwaters is relatively easy, with several options available:

1. **By Air**: The nearest airport is Cochin International Airport (COK), located about 85 km from Alleppey, the hub of the backwaters. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach the backwaters.
2. **By Train**: Alleppey railway station (ALL) is the closest to the backwaters. You can take a train from nearby cities like Trivandrum, Kochi, or Ernakulam to Alleppey.
3. **By Bus**: Alleppey is well-connected to major cities in Kerala and other states by bus. You can take a bus from Kochi, Trivandrum, or other nearby cities to reach the backwaters.

**Top Places to Visit**

The Kerala backwaters offer a wealth of experiences, with numerous places to explore:

1. **Alleppey**: The hub of the backwaters, Alleppey is a bustling town with a rich culture and history. Visit the Alleppey Beach, explore the local markets, and take a boat cruise to experience the serenity of the backwaters.
2. **Kumarakom**: A popular tourist destination, Kumarakom is known for its picturesque backwaters, wildlife, and birdwatching opportunities. Take a boat cruise to spot dolphins, monitor lizards, and numerous bird species.
3. **Mararikulam**: A charming fishing village, Mararikulam offers a glimpse into traditional Kerala life. Visit the Mararikulam Beach, explore the local markets, and take a boat cruise to experience the tranquility of the backwaters.
4. **Pathiramanal**: A small island in the Punnamada Lake, Pathiramanal is famous for its beautiful beaches and birdlife. Take a boat cruise to explore the island and its surroundings.
5. **Punnamada**: A picturesque village located on the shores of the Vembanad Lake, Punnamada is a great place to experience rural Kerala life. Take a boat cruise to explore the lake and its surroundings.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

To experience the beauty of Kerala's backwaters, consider a 7-day itinerary:

* Day 1: Arrive at Alleppey, check-in to a backwater resort, and take a leisurely boat cruise to explore the surroundings.
* Day 2: Visit the Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, take a boat cruise to spot dolphins and monitor lizards, and explore the local markets.
* Day 3: Visit the Mararikulam Beach, explore the local markets, and take a boat cruise to experience the tranquility of the backwaters.
* Day 4: Take a boat cruise to Pathiramanal, explore the island, and spot birdlife.
* Day 5: Visit the Punnamada Lake, take a boat cruise to explore the lake and its surroundings, and experience rural Kerala life.
* Day 6: Visit the Alappuzha Beach, explore the local markets, and take a boat cruise to explore the backwaters.
* Day 7: Depart for Cochin International Airport or return to your hometown.

**Travel Tips**

To make the most of your Kerala backwaters experience:

1. **Book accommodations in advance**: Choose a resorts or hotels located near the backwaters to minimize travel time.
2. **Take a boat cruise**: A boat cruise is the best way to experience the backwaters, as it allows you to spot wildlife, explore the surroundings, and enjoy the tranquility of the water.
3. **Be prepared**: Wear comfortable clothing, bring sunscreen, and stay hydrated to enjoy your boat cruise and outdoor activities.
4. **Respect local culture**: Be respectful of local customs, traditions, and wildlife.

**Budget Tips**

To save money on your Kerala backwaters trip:

1. **Budget-friendly accommodations**: Choose budget-friendly accommodations, such as guesthouses or homestays, to save on costs.
2. **Local transportation**: Use local transportation, such as buses or auto-rickshaws, to travel between places.
3. **Street food**: Eat street food or local cuisine to save on food costs.
4. **Avoid peak season**: Avoid visiting during peak season (December to January) to save on costs.

**Conclusion**

Exploring Kerala's backwaters is an unforgettable experience that offers a glimpse into rural Kerala life, tranquility, and natural beauty. With its picturesque landscapes, lush vegetation, and unique ecosystem, the backwaters are a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. By following our travel guide, you'll be well-prepared to experience the serenity of Kerala's backwaters and make unforgettable memories. So, plan your trip, book your accommodations, and get ready to explore the beauty of Kerala's backwaters!
