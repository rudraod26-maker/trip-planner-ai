
# Gangtok travel guide

**Discover the Hidden Gem of Northeast India: A Gangtok Travel Guide**

**Introduction**

Located in the lap of the Himalayas, Gangtok is the charming capital of Sikkim, a state in Northeast India. This picturesque hill station has been a favorite among travelers and adventure-seekers for decades, offering breathtaking views of the majestic mountains, serene lakes, and lush green forests. If you're planning a trip to North East India, Gangtok is a must-visit destination that will leave you enchanted and inspired. In this comprehensive travel guide, we'll take you through the best time to visit, how to reach, top places to visit, day-wise itinerary, travel tips, and budget tips to make your Gangtok adventure a truly unforgettable experience.

**Best Time to Visit**

The best time to visit Gangtok is from March to May and September to November, when the weather is mild and clear. The summer months (June to August) can be quite hot, while the winters (December to February) can be chilly. During the monsoon season (July to September), the roads can be slippery, making it difficult to navigate.

**How to Reach**

Gangtok is well-connected to major cities in India by air, road, and rail. Here are the different ways to reach Gangtok:

- **By Air:** Bagdogra Airport (IXB) is the nearest airport to Gangtok, located about 124 kilometers away. You can take a taxi or bus from the airport to reach Gangtok.
- **By Train:** New Jalpaiguri Railway Station (NJP) is the nearest railway station to Gangtok, located about 125 kilometers away. You can take a taxi or bus from the station to reach Gangtok.
- **By Road:** Gangtok is connected to major cities in North East India by road. You can take a bus or taxi from Siliguri, Darjeeling, or other nearby towns to reach Gangtok.

**Top Places to Visit**

Gangtok is a treasure trove of natural beauty, with numerous attractions that cater to different interests and ages. Here are some of the top places to visit in Gangtok:

- **Tsomgo Lake:** A beautiful high-altitude lake located at an altitude of 3,780 meters, Tsomgo Lake is a must-visit attraction in Gangtok. Take a stroll around the lake, enjoy the breathtaking views, and soak in the serene atmosphere.
- **Rumtek Monastery:** A stunning Buddhist monastery, Rumtek Monastery is a must-visit attraction in Gangtok. Take a guided tour of the monastery, explore the beautiful gardens, and experience the warm hospitality of the monks.
- **Hanuman Tok:** A beautiful temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman, Hanuman Tok is a must-visit attraction in Gangtok. Enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains, take a stroll around the temple, and soak in the peaceful atmosphere.
- **Namchi Viewpoint:** A stunning viewpoint with breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, Namchi Viewpoint is a must-visit attraction in Gangtok. Take a stroll around the viewpoint, enjoy the stunning views, and capture some wonderful photos.
- **Mangan and Lachen:** Two charming towns located in the Sikkim Himalayas, Mangan and Lachen are must-visit attractions in Gangtok. Explore the beautiful towns, take a stroll around the market, and enjoy the warm hospitality of the locals.

**Day-Wise Itinerary**

Here's a day-wise itinerary to help you plan your trip to Gangtok:

- **Day 1:** arrival at Gangtok, check-in to the hotel, visit the Namchi Viewpoint, and stroll around the local market.
- **Day 2:** visit the Tsomgo Lake, take a guided tour of the Rumtek Monastery, and enjoy the stunning views from the Hanuman Tok.
- **Day 3:** visit the Mangan and Lachen towns, explore the beautiful markets, and enjoy the warm hospitality of the locals.
- **Day 4:** visit the Tenzing Rock, take a stroll around the local market, and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
- **Day 5:** departure from Gangtok.

**Travel Tips**

Here are some travel tips to help you navigate Gangtok with ease:

- **Language:** The official language of Sikkim is Nepali, but Hindi and English are widely spoken in Gangtok. Try to learn some basic phrases in Nepali to communicate with the locals.
- **Currency:** The local currency is the Indian Rupee, and you can easily exchange your currency at the airport or in the local market.
- **Clothing:** Pack comfortable clothing that can withstand the changing weather conditions. Bring warm clothing for the winters and light clothing for the summers.
- **Safety:** Gangtok is generally a safe destination, but take necessary precautions to ensure your safety. Avoid traveling alone at night, and keep your valuables secure.

**Budget Tips**

Here are some budget tips to help you save money in Gangtok:

- **Accommodation:** Gangtok offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Book your accommodation in advance to ensure availability.
- **Food:** Try the local cuisine, which is a delicious mix of Indian and Chinese flavors. Eat at local restaurants or street food stalls to save money.
- **Transportation:** Use public transportation or walk to explore the local markets and attractions.
- **Shopping:** bargaining is a common practice in Sikkim. Try to bargain and buy local handicrafts, clothes, and souvenirs.

**Conclusion**

Gangtok is a hidden gem in Northeast India, offering breathtaking views of the Himalayas, serene lakes, and lush green forests. With its rich history, culture, and natural beauty, Gangtok is a must-visit destination for travel enthusiasts and adventure-seekers. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you'll be able to navigate Gangtok with ease, explore its top attractions, and experience the warm hospitality of the locals. So, pack your bags, book your flights, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in Gangtok!
