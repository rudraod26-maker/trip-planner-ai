
# Jaipur to Jaisalmer road trip guide

**Jaipur to Jaisalmer Road Trip Guide: A Desert Adventure**

Are you ready to embark on an epic road trip adventure through the arid landscape of Rajasthan? Look no further than the enchanting journey from Jaipur to Jaisalmer, a captivating desert destination. This majestic road trip spans over 600 kilometers, taking you through quaint towns, historical forts, and the mesmerizing Sand Dunes of the Thar Desert. In this comprehensive travel guide, we'll cover the best time to visit, how to reach, top places to visit, day-wise itinerary, travel tips, and budget tips to ensure a seamless and unforgettable experience.

**Introduction**

Jaipur to Jaisalmer road trip is a thrilling adventure that offers a unique experience of exploring the vibrant cultures, historical landmarks, and breathtaking landscapes of Rajasthan. As you set out on this incredible journey, you'll be transported to a world of grandeur, opulence, and timeless beauty. With its rich history, vibrant cities, and pristine natural scenery, this road trip is an ideal getaway for those seeking an adrenaline-packed adventure amidst the Indian desert landscape.

**Best Time to Visit**

The best time to embark on this thrilling road trip is from October to February when the weather is pleasant and comfortable. The summer months (March to June) are scorching hot, and the monsoon months (July to September) are best avoided due to heavy rainfall and potential roadblocks.

October to February:

* Pleasant weather with daytime temperatures ranging from 20°C to 28°C (68°F to 82°F)
* Cool night temperatures, ideal for stargazing
* Minimal rainfall, allowing for a hassle-free journey

**How to Reach**

The journey from Jaipur to Jaisalmer can be completed by car, bus, or train. Here are your options:

* **By Car**: Take the NH 12 and NH 15 route, which spans approximately 600 kilometers. The journey takes around 8-10 hours, depending on traffic conditions.
* **By Bus**: Several bus services operate daily from Jaipur to Jaisalmer, including the Rajasthan State Road Transport (RSRTC) and private buses like Volvo, Mercedes-Benz, and Tempo Travellers. The journey takes around 10-12 hours.
* **By Train**: The nearest railway station to Jaisalmer is Jaisalmer Rail Head, which is connected to Jaipur by the Delhi-Ajmer-Jaisalmer Express. The train journey takes around 10-12 hours.

**Top Places to Visit**

Some of the top attractions on this road trip include:

* **Pushkar**: A sacred town situated on the banks of Lake Pushkar, famous for its Brahma Temple and vibrant Pushkar Camel Fair.
* **Jaitaran**: A historic town boasting a stunning 16th-century fort, offering panoramic views of the arid landscape.
* **Jodhpur**: A majestic city, known as the Blue City, featuring the imposing Mehrangarh Fort and the serene Jaswant Thada Cenotaph.
* **Osian**: An ancient town famous for its ornate temples and stunning sunsets.
* **Kuldhara**: An abandoned village with an eerie charm, boasting ancient architecture and a mysterious past.
* **Sam Sand Dunes**: The iconic tourist destination near Jaisalmer, offering breathtaking views of the endless desert and stunning sunsets.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Day 1: Jaipur to Pushkar (145 kilometers, 3-4 hours)

* Arrive at Pushkar and check into your hotel
* Explore the sacred Brahma Temple and witness the bustling Pushkar Camel Fair (seasonal)
* Visit the nearby lake and enjoy a sunset boat ride

Day 2: Pushkar to Jaitaran (110 kilometers, 3-4 hours)

* Depart for Jaitaran and explore the historic fort with its stunning views
* Visit the nearby villages and interact with the locals
* Overnight stay at a local hotel or guesthouse

Day 3: Jaitaran to Jodhpur (130 kilometers, 3-4 hours)

* Arrive in Jodhpur and check into your hotel
* Explore the majestic Mehrangarh Fort and the serene Jaswant Thada Cenotaph
* Visit the bustling bazaars and try some local street food

Day 4: Jodhpur to Osian (120 kilometers, 3 hours)

* Depart for Osian and explore the ancient temples
* Witness a stunning sunset in the desert landscape
* Overnight stay at a local hotel or guesthouse

Day 5: Osian to Sam Sand Dunes (45 kilometers, 1-2 hours)

* Arrive at Sam Sand Dunes and enjoy a camel ride into the sunset
* Witness the breathtaking views of the endless desert
* Overnight stay in a luxury tent or a local hotel

Day 6: Sam Sand Dunes to Jaisalmer (55 kilometers, 2-3 hours)

* Explore the town of Jaisalmer and its majestic fort
* Visit the nearby temples and witness the vibrant culture
* Depart for your return journey

**Travel Tips**

1. **Carry essential items**: Pack a first-aid kit, refillable water bottles, and snacks for the journey.
2. **Choose a reliable vehicle**: Select a car with good suspension and adequate legroom.
3. **Drive safely**: Follow local traffic rules and take regular breaks to avoid fatigue.
4. **Respect local culture**: Be mindful of the local customs and traditions.
5. **Stay hydrated**: Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration in the arid desert climate.

**Budget Tips**

1. **Accommodation**: Choose budget-friendly guesthouses or local hotels.
2. **Food**: Opt for local street food or cook your own meals.
3. **Transportation**: Rent a car with a good fuel economy.
4. **Activities**: Explore free attractions and avoid expensive tours.
5. **Budget planning**: Plan your expenses in advance to avoid unexpected costs.
