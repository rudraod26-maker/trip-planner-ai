
# Desert safari Jaisalmer guide

**Desert Safari Jaisalmer Guide: Unwind Under the Golden Sand Dunes**

Located in the westernmost part of India, Jaisalmer is a city steeped in history and mystery, a fusion of ancient cultures that has given birth to a magical landscape. The Thar Desert, a stretch of golden sand dunes that seems to stretch on forever, is the backdrop to Jaisalmer's Desert Safari. Whether you are a thrill-seeker, an adventure enthusiast, or a soul searching traveler, Jaisalmer Desert Safari has something for everyone.

**Introduction**

Jaisalmer Desert Safari is an unmissable experience that offers breathtaking views of the Thar Desert, an exhilarating ride through sand dunes, and the warmth of a Rajasthani welcome. This two-hour adventure takes you on a thrilling ride through the desert on camelback or by SUVs, giving you a taste of the nomadic culture that has thrived on the outskirts of Jaisalmer for centuries.

**Best Time to Visit**

The ideal time to visit Jaisalmer and experience the Desert Safari is between October and February, when the weather is relatively calm and sunny. The summer months can get scorching hot, reaching temperatures of up to 45-degree Celsius, while the monsoon season is a time of heavy rainfall and flash floods. The winter months of October and November offer the perfect blend of sunshine and cool breezes, making it an ideal time to explore the desert and experience the Desert Safari to its fullest.

**How to Reach**

Getting to Jaisalmer from any part of the country is relatively easy and hassle-free. Here are a few options:

* **By Air:** The closest airport to Jaisalmer is Jaisalmer Airport, which receives flights from major cities such as Delhi and Jaipur. You can also fly into Jodhpur airport, which is about 290 km from Jaisalmer, or Mumbai airport, which is about 900 km.
* **By Train:** Jaisalmer railway station is well-connected to major cities across India, including Delhi, Mumbai, and Jaipur. Trains are a convenient and cost-effective way to reach Jaisalmer.
* **By Road:** Jaisalmer is connected to all major cities by road. The most convenient way to reach Jaisalmer is by bus or car, which offers stunning views of the desert landscape along the way.

**Top Places to Visit**

Jaisalmer is a treasure trove of ancient forts, palaces, and temples, each one offering a glimpse into the rich history and culture of Rajasthan. Here are a few must-visit places to experience the essence of Jaisalmer:

* **Jaisalmer Fort:** Also known as Sonar Fort, this 9th-century fort is a marvel of Rajasthani architecture and engineering. The fort complex houses a temple, several shops, and accommodation for tourists.
* **Patwon Ki Haveli:** One of the most exquisite havelis in Jaisalmer, Patwon Ki Haveli is a testament to Rajasthan's grandeur and opulence.
* **Sam Sand Dunes:** The perfect spot to catch the sunset, Sam Sand Dunes offers breathtaking views of the sand dunes, stretching as far as the eye can see.
* **Gadisar Lake:** This serene lake, located just outside the city walls, is a tranquil oasis in the middle of the desert.
* **Thar Desert Museum:** A must-visit for anyone interested in the history and culture of Rajasthan, this museum showcases an impressive collection of artifacts and exhibits.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Here's a suggested day-wise itinerary to experience the best of Jaisalmer and its Desert Safari:

Day 1: Arrival and Exploration
* Arrive in Jaisalmer and check into a hotel.
* Visit Jaisalmer Fort and explore the surrounding streets.
* Visit Patwon Ki Haveli and admire its intricate designs.
* End the day with a stroll along the Gadisar Lake.

Day 2: Desert Safari
* Set out on a Desert Safari, riding a camel or SUV through the sand dunes.
* Watch the sunrise over the desert and enjoy a traditional Rajasthani breakfast.
* Visit the Thar Desert Museum and learn about the history and culture of the region.
* Return to Jaisalmer in the evening and enjoy a traditional Rajasthani dinner.

Day 3: Exploring the Desert
* Visit Sam Sand Dunes and watch the sunset over the sand dunes.
* Take a camel ride through the desert and enjoy the tranquility of the landscape.
* Visit a nearby village and learn about the daily lives of the locals.
* Return to Jaisalmer in the evening and enjoy a relaxing dinner.

**Travel Tips**

Here are a few travel tips to make your Desert Safari experience more enjoyable:

* **Book in Advance:** Book your Desert Safari and accommodation in advance to avoid last-minute hassles.
* **Wear Comfortable Shoes:** Wear comfortable shoes and dress according to the weather to make the most of your Desert Safari.
* **Bring Sun Protection:** Bring sun protection, including sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat to protect yourself from the harsh desert sun.
* **Respect the Locals:** Respect the local culture and traditions, and avoid taking pictures of people without their permission.

**Budget Tips**

Here are a few budget tips to help you plan your Desert Safari:

* **Booking in Advance:** Booking your Desert Safari and accommodation in advance can save you up to 20% on the total cost.
* **Choose Affordable Accommodation:** Choose affordable accommodation options, such as guesthouses or hostels, to save money.
* **Avoid Tourist Traps:** Avoid tourist traps and opt for local eateries and shops to get the best deals.
* **Negotiate:** Negotiate prices with local shopkeepers and sellers to get the best deals.
