
# Kerala travel guide

**Unveiling the Gems of Kerala: A Travel Guide to India's Tropical Paradise**

Located in the southernmost part of India, Kerala is a treasure trove of breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and serene atmosphere. Often referred to as "God's Own Country," Kerala is a popular destination for travelers seeking a perfect blend of relaxation, adventure, and spiritual rejuvenation. In this comprehensive guide, we'll delve into the best time to visit, how to reach, top places to visit, and offer expert tips to help you plan an unforgettable trip to this beautiful Indian state.

**Best Time to Visit Kerala**

Kerala is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences and the activities you have planned. The state experiences a tropical monsoon climate, with three distinct seasons:

1. **Winter (October to February)**: This is the best time to visit Kerala, with mild temperatures (20-25°C) and low humidity, making it ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.
2. **Summer (March to May)**: Summer is the dry season in Kerala, with high temperatures (25-35°C) and minimal rainfall. This is a good time to explore the hill stations and enjoy water sports.
3. **Monsoon (June to September)**: Monsoon brings heavy rainfall, making some attractions inaccessible. However, this is a great time to experience the lush green landscape, and take part in activities like trekking and boat cruises.

**How to Reach Kerala**

Reaching Kerala is relatively easy, with various transportation options available:

1. **By Air**: Kerala has three international airports – Cochin International Airport (COK), Thiruvananthapuram International Airport (TRV), and Calicut International Airport (CCJ). You can fly from major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore, or take connecting flights from international destinations.
2. **By Rail**: Kerala has an extensive rail network, with trains connecting major cities like Thiruvananthapuram, Kottayam, and Ernakulam. You can take an overnight train from Delhi or Mumbai to Ernakulam.
3. **By Road**: Kerala is well-connected by road, with buses and private taxis available from major cities like Delhi, Bangalore, and Chennai. Driving is a popular option, as it offers scenic routes and flexibility.

**Top Places to Visit in Kerala**

Kerala is a treasure trove of attractions, ranging from beaches and backwaters to temples and wildlife sanctuaries. Here are some of the top places to visit:

1. **Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary**: Home to elephants, tigers, and other endangered species, this sanctuary offers a thrilling experience for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.
2. **Munnar**: A hill station famous for its tea plantations, scenic views, and picturesque valleys.
3. **Alleppey**: Known as the "Venice of the East," Alleppey is a must-visit for its serene backwaters, floating markets, and houseboat stays.
4. **Kerala Backwaters**: Kerala's labyrinthine waterways offer a unique experience, with boat cruises and homestays available.
5. **Thiruvalla Temple**: A prominent temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu, showcasing intricate carvings and stunning architecture.
6. **Trivandrum**: The state capital, offering a rich cultural heritage, stunning beaches, and a vibrant atmosphere.
7. **Fort Kochi**: A historic port city with colonial legacy, colorful architecture, and a thriving art scene.

**Day-wise Itinerary for Kerala**

To make the most of your trip, here's a suggested day-wise itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival in Kochi, visit Kochi Fort and St. Francis Church

Day 2: Explore Fort Kochi, visit Chinese Fishing Nets, and take a ferry ride to nearby islands

Day 3: Visit Alappuzha (Alleppey), take a boat ride on the backwaters, and stay overnight in a houseboat

Day 4: Drive to Munnar (approx. 100 km), visit Mattupetti Dam, and soak in the breathtaking views

Day 5: Explore Eravikulam National Park, visit the Tea Plantations, and take a leisurely drive through the hills

Day 6-7: Visit Kovalam Beach, take a boat ride, and indulge in water sports

**Travel Tips for Kerala**

To have a smooth and enjoyable trip, follow these expert tips:

1. **Dress Modestly**: Dress conservatively when visiting temples and other cultural sites.
2. **Respect Local Customs**: Be respectful of local traditions and customs, especially during festivals and ceremonies.
3. **Hire a Guide**: Hire a knowledgeable guide to help navigate the state's rich history and cultural heritage.
4. **Try Local Cuisine**: Sample the authentic Keralite cuisine, which is influenced by Indian, Arabic, and Chinese flavors.
5. **Use Eco-Friendly Transportation**: Use eco-friendly transportation options like bikes or electric autos for a carbon-neutral travel experience.

**Budget Tips for Kerala**

Kerala is relatively affordable, with a wide range of accommodation options to suit various budgets:

1. **Accommodation**: Stay in guesthouses, homestays, or luxury resorts to suit your budget.
2. **Transportation**: Use buses, taxis, or rent a self-drive bike or car to get around the state.
3. **Food**: Enjoy local street food, eat at mid-range restaurants, or dine at high-end restaurants for a memorable dining experience.
4. **Activities**: Explore government-approved trekking routes, take part in cultural festivals, and participate in traditional Ayurvedic massages.

**Conclusion**

Kerala is an enchanting destination that offers a serene atmosphere, rich cultural heritage, and breathtaking landscapes. Whether you're seeking relaxation, adventure, or spiritual rejuvenation, Kerala has something to offer everyone. With this travel guide, you'll be well-prepared for an unforgettable journey through the "God's Own Country." So, pack your bags, grab your sunscreen, and get ready to experience the enchanting world of Kerala.
