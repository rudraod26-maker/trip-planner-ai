
# Mathura Vrindavan itinerary

**Discover the Spiritual Heart of India: A Comprehensive Mathura Vrindavan Itinerary**

Located in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India, Mathura and Vrindavan are two sacred cities that hold immense significance for Hindus worldwide. Known for their rich history, spiritual essence, and breathtaking architecture, these cities have been beckoning pilgrims and travelers alike for centuries. In this article, we will guide you through the best time to visit, how to reach, top places to visit, and a detailed day-wise itinerary for a memorable Mathura Vrindavan trip.

**Introduction**

Mathura, often referred to as the 'City of Lord Krishna', is the birthplace of the divine prince who is revered as an avatar of the Lord Vishnu. Vrindavan, a neighboring town, is the place where Lord Krishna spent his childhood and performed numerous miracles. The two cities are intertwined with the life of Lord Krishna, making them a must-visit destination for devotees and history enthusiasts. Together, they offer a spiritual journey that is both rejuvenating and awe-inspiring.

**Best Time to Visit**

The best time to visit Mathura and Vrindavan is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant, cool, and dry. The summer season, from April to June, can be scorching hot, making it difficult to explore the cities. The monsoon season, from July to September, is also not ideal, as some of the temples and ghats may be closed due to heavy rainfall. Avoid visiting during the peak summer months if you want to make the most of your trip.

**How to Reach**

Mathura and Vrindavan are well-connected to major cities in India, making it easy to reach. Here are the different ways to reach these cities:

* **By Air**: The nearest airport is the Agra Airport, which is approximately 45 kilometers away from Mathura. You can take a private taxi or public transport to reach Mathura.
* **By Train**: Mathura has a railway station, which is a major halt for several trains. You can take a direct train from Delhi, Agra, or other major cities in India.
* **By Road**: Mathura is connected to Agra, Delhi, and other nearby cities through a well-maintained highway network. You can hire a private taxi or take public transport, such as buses, to reach Mathura.

**Top Places to Visit**

Mathura and Vrindavan are filled with numerous temples, ghats, and historical sites that are a testament to their rich heritage. Here are some of the top places to visit:

* **ISKCON Temple**: This magnificent temple complex is a must-visit destination for devotees of Lord Krishna. The temple is built in the style of a medieval Indian palace and offers a glimpse into the life of Lord Krishna.
* **Banke Bihari Temple**: This ancient temple is one of the most famous temples in Vrindavan and is dedicated to Lord Krishna. The temple is known for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings.
* **Dwarkadheesh Temple**: This temple is one of the largest temples in Vrindavan and is dedicated to Lord Krishna. The temple is known for its beautiful idol of Lord Krishna and its stunning architecture.
* **Gita Mandir**: This temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and the Bhagavad Gita. The temple is known for its stunning architecture and its beautiful surroundings.
* **Seva Kunj**: This place is said to be the site where Lord Krishna spent his childhood days. The area is filled with several temples and is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

To make the most of your trip to Mathura and Vrindavan, here is a suggested day-wise itinerary:

**Day 1: Arrival and Explore Mathura**

* Arrive at Mathura and check into your hotel.
* Visit the ISKCON Temple, one of the most famous temples in Mathura.
* Explore the nearby area, which includes the Dwarkadheesh Temple and the Banke Bihari Temple.
* Return to your hotel and rest for the night.

**Day 2: Explore Vrindavan**

* Start your day with a visit to the Banke Bihari Temple, one of the most famous temples in Vrindavan.
* Explore the nearby area, which includes the Dwarkadheesh Temple and the Gita Mandir.
* Visit the Seva Kunj, the site where Lord Krishna spent his childhood days.
* Return to your hotel and rest for the night.

**Day 3: Ghats and Temples**

* Start your day with a visit to the Yamuna Ghat, one of the most famous ghats in Vrindavan.
* Explore the nearby area, which includes the Radha Raman Temple and the Narsingh Temple.
* Visit the Radha Damodar Temple, one of the oldest temples in Vrindavan.
* Return to your hotel and rest for the night.

**Travel Tips**

* **Dress modestly**: When visiting temples and ghats, make sure to dress modestly, covering your shoulders and legs.
* **Respect local customs**: Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples and ghats.
* **Stay hydrated**: Mathura and Vrindavan can get extremely hot during the summer months, so make sure to stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.
* **Hire a guide**: Hire a local guide to help you navigate the cities and temples, which can be overwhelming at times.

**Budget Tips**

* **Accommodation**: Mathura and Vrindavan have a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels.
* **Food**: The local cuisine in Mathura and Vrindavan is delicious and affordable. Try the local dishes, such as dal-bhaat and poori-shahi, which are available at a reasonable price.
* **Transportation**: The cities have a well-maintained transportation system, including buses and auto-rickshaws, which are affordable and convenient.
