
# Delhi to Leh road trip guide

**Delhi to Leh Road Trip Guide: A Journey Through the Unspoiled Beauty of Ladakh**

The Delhi to Leh road trip is a thrilling adventure that attracts travelers from all over the world. This challenging yet rewarding journey takes you through the scenic landscapes, historical landmarks, and vibrant cultures of the Indian Himalayas. In this article, we'll guide you through everything you need to know to embark on this epic journey.

**Introduction**

The Delhi to Leh road trip is a 473-kilometer journey that spans across the Indian states of Himachal Pradesh and Jammu and Kashmir. The route takes you through some of the most breathtaking landscapes, including the stunning Khardung La pass, the world's highest motorable road. The trip is an absolute must for adventure seekers, nature lovers, and those looking to experience the real India.

**Best Time to Visit**

The best time to visit Ladakh is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, making it ideal for road trips. The temperatures during this period range from 15°C to 25°C, making it perfect for hiking, camping, and sightseeing. However, be prepared for the monsoon season from July to August, which can lead to sudden rain showers and landslides.

**How to Reach**

To reach Leh from Delhi, you'll need to take a series of buses, taxis, or private vehicles. Here are the best options:

1. **By Air:** The nearest airport to Leh is Kushok Bakula Rinpoche Airport, also known as the Leh Airport. You can fly to Leh from Delhi, which takes about 1 hour and 30 minutes. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach the center of the city.
2. **By Road:** You can take buses or private vehicles from Delhi to Leh via the Manali-Leh highway. The journey takes around 48-72 hours, depending on the road conditions and your mode of transportation.
3. **By Bus:** Regular bus services are available from Delhi to Leh, run by private bus operators and the Himachal Pradesh State Transport Department.

**Top Places to Visit**

Here are some of the top places to visit on your Delhi to Leh road trip:

1. **Manali:** A bustling hill station in Himachal Pradesh, known for its scenic landscapes, adventure activities, and vibrant culture.
2. **Rohtang Pass:** A high-altitude mountain pass that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
3. **Jispa:** A charming village in Himachal Pradesh, known for its scenic beauty and adventure activities like trekking, camping, and fishing.
4. **Sarchu:** A picturesque village in Himachal Pradesh, known for its stunning landscapes, trekking trails, and camping sites.
5. **Nubra Valley:** A scenic valley in Ladakh, known for its sand dunes, scenic vistas, and adventure activities like camel riding and trekking.
6. **Pangong Tso Lake:** A picturesque lake in Ladakh, known for its stunning beauty, adventure activities like boating and fishing, and breathtaking sunsets.
7. **Khardung La Pass:** The world's highest motorable road, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

**Day-Wise Itinerary**

Here's a suggested day-wise itinerary for your Delhi to Leh road trip:

Day 1: **Delhi to Manali (550 km, 10-12 hours)**

* Start early in the morning from Delhi and take a bus or drive to Manali.
* Visit the Hadimba Devi Temple, Vashisht Temple, and the Old Manali village.
* Overnight stay in Manali.

Day 2: **Manali to Rohtang Pass and Jispa (170 km, 6-8 hours)**

* Drive to the Rohtang Pass (13,050 ft) for breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
* Drive to Jispa (12,500 ft) for an overnight stay.
* Explore the Jispa village and its scenic surroundings.

Day 3: **Jispa to Sarchu (210 km, 6-8 hours)**

* Drive to Sarchu (14,070 ft) for breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
* Explore the Sarchu village and its scenic surroundings.
* Overnight stay in Sarchu.

Day 4: **Sarchu to Nubra Valley (210 km, 6-8 hours)**

* Drive to Nubra Valley (11,000 ft) for scenic views of the sand dunes.
* Explore the Nubra Valley village and its scenic surroundings.
* Overnight stay in Nubra Valley.

Day 5: **Nubra Valley to Pangong Tso Lake (175 km, 6-8 hours)**

* Drive to Pangong Tso Lake (14,271 ft) for breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
* Explore the Pangong Tso Lake village and its scenic surroundings.
* Overnight stay in Pangong Tso Lake.

Day 6: **Pangong Tso Lake to Leh (170 km, 6-8 hours)**

* Drive to Leh (11,000 ft) for a restful day.
* Explore the Leh town and its scenic surroundings.
* Overnight stay in Leh.

Day 7: **Leh to Khardung La Pass (40 km, 2-3 hours)**

* Drive to Khardung La Pass (18,380 ft) for breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
* Explore the Khardung La Pass village and its scenic surroundings.
* Overnight stay in Leh.

**Travel Tips**

Here are some essential travel tips for your Delhi to Leh road trip:

1. **Check the weather forecast:** Before embarking on your journey, check the weather forecast to ensure that the roads are clear and safe for travel.
2. **Get a good vehicle:** Hire a reliable vehicle that can handle high-altitude roads.
3. **Pack layers:** Pack clothing that can be layered to adapt to changing weather conditions.
4. **Bring essentials:** Bring essentials like water, snacks, and first-aid kits to keep you safe on the road.
5. **Drive carefully:** Drive carefully and follow traffic rules to avoid accidents.
6. **Respect local culture:** Respect local culture and traditions when visiting villages and towns.

**Budget Tips**

Here are some budget tips for your Delhi to Leh road trip:

2. **Cook your own meals:** Cook your own meals to save money on food.
3. **Avoid luxury accommodations:** Avoid luxury accommodations and opt for budget-friendly options.
