
# Nainital 3 day itinerary

**Discover the Charm of Nainital: A 3-Day Itinerary**

Nestled in the foothills of the Kumaon Himalayas, Nainital is a stunning hill station in the Indian state of Uttarakhand. This picturesque town is known for its lush green forests, sparkling lakes, and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. As a popular tourist destination, Nainital attracts visitors from all over the world, offering a range of activities and attractions that cater to different interests. In this travel guide, we will explore the best time to visit Nainital, how to reach there, top places to visit, a day-wise itinerary, travel tips, and budget tips to make your trip a memorable one.

**Best Time to Visit**

Nainital is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from March to June and from September to November. These periods offer mild weather, making it ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities. The summer months (April to June) are perfect for boating, trekking, and hiking, while the winter months (October to November) are ideal for skiing and snowboarding.

During the peak season (July to August), Nainital receives heavy rainfall, which can make the roads and trails slippery and treacherous. However, the lush green forests and blooming flowers make it a stunning sight.

**How to Reach**

Nainital is well-connected to major cities in India by road, rail, and air. Here are the ways to reach Nainital:

* **By Air**: The nearest airport is the Pantnagar Airport, which is approximately 72 km from Nainital and takes about 2 hours to reach. You can also fly into Delhi International Airport (DEL) and then take a taxi or bus to Nainital.
* **By Rail**: The nearest railway station is the Kathgodam railway station, which is approximately 35 km from Nainital and takes about 1 hour to reach. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to Nainital.
* **By Road**: Nainital is connected to major cities like Delhi, Mussoorie, and Shimla through National Highway 93. You can hire a taxi or take a bus from ISBT Anand Vihar in Delhi.

**Top Places to Visit**

Nainital is a treasure trove of natural beauty, offering a range of attractions that will leave you enchanted. Here are some of the top places to visit in Nainital:

* **Naini Lake**: The star attraction of Nainital, Naini Lake is a picturesque spot for boating, fishing, and sunset viewing.
* **Tiffin Top**: Also known as Dorothy's Seat, Tiffin Top offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and the Naini Lake.
* **Snow View Point**: This vantage point offers stunning views of the snow-capped peaks of the Nanda Devi range.
* **Eagle's Nest**: This spot offers panoramic views of the Naini Lake and the surrounding mountains.
* **High Altitude Zoo**: Located at an altitude of 2,100 meters, this zoo is home to a range of animals like deer, bears, and birds.
* **Sattal**: A scenic lake surrounded by seven hills, Sattal is a peaceful spot for boating and picnicking.
* **Nainital Mall Road**: This bustling shopping street offers a range of shopping and dining options.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Here's a suggested 3-day itinerary for your trip to Nainital:

**Day 1: Naini Lake and Surroundings**

* Morning: Visit the Naini Lake and take a boat ride on the lake.
* Afternoon: Explore the Tiffin Top and the Snow View Point.
* Evening: Enjoy the sunset at the Naini Lake and try some local street food.

**Day 2: Sattal and nearby attractions**

* Morning: Visit the Sattal and take a boat ride on the lake.
* Afternoon: Explore the Eagle's Nest and the High Altitude Zoo.
* Evening: Return to Nainital and enjoy the evening shopping on the Mall Road.

**Day 3: Trekking and adventure activities**

* Morning: Take a trek to the nearby village of Lohajung or the Aridhodi Village.
* Afternoon: Engage in adventure activities like paragliding, skydiving, or zip-lining.
* Evening: Return to Nainital and enjoy a relaxed evening at your hotel.

**Travel Tips**

Here are some useful travel tips for your trip to Nainital:

* **Weather**: Pack warm clothing for the winter months and umbrellas for the rainy season.
* **Road Condition**: Be cautious while driving in the hills and take regular breaks to avoid fatigue.
* **Altitude Sickness**: Drink plenty of water and take rest if you feel any symptoms of altitude sickness.
* **Language**: Hindi and English are widely spoken in Nainital, but it's always helpful to learn some basic phrases in local languages.

**Budget Tips**

Here are some budget-friendly tips for your trip to Nainital:

* **Accommodation**: Stay at budget-friendly hotels or guest houses.
* **Food**: Try local street food and restaurants that offer affordable meals.
* **Transportation**: Use public transport or hike and trek to nearby attractions.
* **Activities**: Engage in free activities like walking and sightseeing.

**Conclusion**

Nainital is a charming hill station that offers a range of attractions and activities for all interests. By following this 3-day itinerary, you'll be able to experience the best of Nainital. Remember to plan ahead, be prepared for the weather, and be respectful of the local culture and environment. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and warm hospitality, Nainital is a must-visit destination for any travel enthusiast.
