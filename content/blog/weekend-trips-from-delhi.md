
# Weekend trips from Delhi

**Weekend Getaways from Delhi: Explore India's Hidden Gems**

Delhi, the capital city of India, is a bustling metropolis that's home to over 29 million people. While it's a great place to live and work, sometimes you just need to escape the chaos and unwind in nature's lap. That's where weekend getaways from Delhi come into play. In this blog, we'll explore some of the best weekend trips from Delhi, including top places to visit, how to reach, and day-wise itineraries.

**Best Time to Visit**

The best time to visit Delhi and its surrounding areas depends on the season. The city experiences a humid subtropical climate with extreme temperatures. The summer season (April to June) can be scorching hot, while the winters (December to February) are pleasant. Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) are the ideal times to visit as the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

**How to Reach**

Delhi is well-connected to the rest of India by air, road, and rail. You can easily reach Delhi by plane, bus, or train from various cities across the country. To reach the nearby weekend getaway destinations, you can hire a taxi or drive your own car, or use public transportation such as buses and trains.

**Top Places to Visit**

Here are some of the top places to visit on a weekend getaway from Delhi:

1. **Agra** (200 km, 3-4 hours)
Agra is home to the iconic Taj Mahal, one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Visit the Taj Mahal at sunrise or sunset to experience its breathtaking beauty.
2. **Mathura and Vrindavan** (180 km, 3-4 hours)
Mathura and Vrindavan are holy cities located on the banks of the Yamuna River. Visit the Govardhan Hill, the birthplace of Lord Krishna, and explore the temples and ghats of Vrindavan.
3. **Jaipur** (285 km, 5-6 hours)
Jaipur, the Pink City, is a stunning example of Rajasthani architecture. Visit the Amber Fort, the City Palace, and the Jantar Mantar Observatory to explore the city's rich history.
4. **Mussoorie** (280 km, 6-7 hours)
Mussoorie is a picturesque hill station surrounded by mountains and valleys. Take a ropeway ride to the Mall Road, visit the Lal Tibba viewpoint, and enjoy the stunning views of the city.
5. **Rishikesh** (230 km, 5-6 hours)
Rishikesh is a spiritual hub located on the banks of the Ganges River. Visit the Lakshman Jhula, the Ram Jhula, and the Tapti Kund, and enjoy the scenic beauty of the surrounding hills.
6. **Nainital** (320 km, 5-6 hours)
Nainital is a beautiful hill station surrounded by lakes and mountains. Visit the Naina Devi Temple, the Naini Lake, and the Snow View Point to enjoy the stunning views of the city.
7. **Haridwar** (210 km, 4-5 hours)
Haridwar is a sacred city located on the banks of the Ganges River. Visit the Har ki Pauri, the Vishnu Ghat, and the Chandi Devi Temple to explore the city's spiritual significance.
8. **Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary** (50 km, 1-2 hours)
Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary is a haven for birdwatchers and nature lovers. Visit the sanctuary to spot various species of birds and enjoy the scenic beauty of the surrounding wetlands.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Here's a sample day-wise itinerary for a weekend getaway from Delhi:

**Option 1: Agra**

- Day 1: Reach Agra in the morning (200 km, 3-4 hours) and visit the Taj Mahal at sunrise. Explore the Red Fort and the Itmad-Ud-Daula's Tomb in the afternoon.
- Day 2: Visit the Fatehpur Sikri and the Akbar's Tomb in the morning. Return to Delhi in the evening (200 km, 3-4 hours).

**Option 2: Mathura and Vrindavan**

- Day 1: Reach Mathura and Vrindavan in the afternoon (180 km, 3-4 hours). Visit the Govardhan Hill and the temples of Vrindavan in the evening.
- Day 2: Explore the ghats of Mathura, visit the Krishna Janmabhoomi, and return to Delhi in the evening (180 km, 3-4 hours).

**Option 3: Jaipur**

- Day 1: Reach Jaipur in the morning (285 km, 5-6 hours) and visit the Amber Fort in the afternoon. Explore the City Palace and the Jantar Mantar Observatory in the evening.
- Day 2: Visit the Hawa Mahal and the Johri Bazar in the morning. Return to Delhi in the evening (285 km, 5-6 hours).

**Travel Tips**

- Plan your trip in advance to avoid the rush and heat.
- Pack light and comfortable clothing and footwear.
- Bring sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
- Try local cuisine and drinks to experience the culture of the place.
- Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting sacred sites.
- Hire a licensed taxi or drive your own car to ensure safety on the road.

**Budget Tips**

- Book your accommodation and transportation in advance to get the best prices.
- Opt for budget-friendly options like guesthouses and homestays instead of luxury hotels.
- Eat at local eateries and street food stalls to save money and experience the local cuisine.
- Avoid buying expensive souvenirs and instead opt for handmade crafts and local handicrafts.
- Use public transportation or walk to save on transportation costs.

**Conclusion**

Delhi is a bustling metropolis that offers plenty of opportunities for weekend getaways. From the stunning Taj Mahal in Agra to the picturesque hill stations of Mussoorie and Nainital, there are plenty of options to choose from. Whether you're interested in history, spirituality, or nature, Delhi has something to offer. Plan your weekend getaway from Delhi today and experience the beauty of India!
