
# Kedarnath travel guide

**Kedarnath Travel Guide: A Holistic Adventure in the Himalayas**

Tucked away in the majestic Garhwal Himalayas, Kedarnath is a pilgrimage town in the Indian state of Uttarakhand, renowned for its enchanting beauty and spiritual significance. Located at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 ft), this sacred place is a destination that enthralls both devout travelers and trek enthusiasts alike. As the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas stand guard over the town, the serene ambiance and the majestic sight of the majestic temple nestled amidst the dense forests and lush green meadows leave an eternal impression on one's soul.

**Best Time to Visit**

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable journey to Kedarnath, it is vital to know the best time to visit. The ideal time to pay a visit to this magnificent destination is from April to June and September to November.

* **Spring (April-May)**: The spring season offers a picturesque setting with lush greenery and a profusion of wildflowers. The weather is usually pleasant with a relatively cool temperature, ranging from 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F).
* **Autumn (September-November)**: Autumn is another excellent time to visit Kedarnath. The skies are clear, the atmosphere is cool, and the lush green meadows have transformed into a kaleidoscope of vibrant colors. 

If you are a devotee or want to perform the Kedarnath yatra, consider visiting during the months of May and June or September and October.

**How to Reach**

Kedarnath is well-connected by various modes of transport from nearby cities as well as from distant places:

* **By Air:** The nearest airport is the Jolly Grant Airport (DEDR) in Dehradun, from where you can take a taxi or rent a cab to Rishikesh, Haridwar, or any nearby places. Alternatively, you can fly to the nearest airport, the Pantnagar Airport (PGH) in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand.
* **By Train:** The nearest railway station is Rishikesh (10 km/6.2 miles from Haridwar), but you can also take a train to Dehradun, which is a larger city with more connectivity options. From there, you can catch a bus or taxi to reach Rishikesh, which is the major base for Kedarnath yatra.
* **By Road:** You can also reach Kedarnath by driving from major cities like Haridwar (220 km/136.6 miles) or Rishikesh (240 km/150 miles). The road conditions vary from smooth to difficult, and it might take around 6-8 hours of driving from Haridwar.

**Top Places to Visit**

Here are the must-visit places around Kedarnath:

* **Kedarnath Temple**: This iconic temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters (11,755 ft). It is one of the 12 jyotirlingas and the 5th dhams of the panch kedar.
* **Guptkashi**: This charming town is the base for Kedarnath yatra and boasts several beautiful temples, bazaars, and natural scenery. Don't miss the majestic temple of Ardhnarishwar.
* **Tungnath Temple**: Located at an altitude of 3,895 meters (12,745 ft), this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is the second highest temple in the world. It serves as a great base for adventure activities such as skiing, climbing, and hiking.
* **Deoria Tal**: This stunning lake is a perfect picnic spot and is famous for its breathtaking natural scenery and stunning trekking trails.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Follow this day-wise itinerary to make the most of your Kedarnath trip:

Day 1: Arrival at Rishikesh:

* Arrive at Rishikesh and check-in to your hotel.
* Visit the nearby sacred temples and ashrams.
* Explore the bustling streets of Rishikesh for shopping and local delicacies.

Day 2: Rishikesh - Haridwar - Guptkashi:

* Board the bus or taxi from Rishikesh to Guptkashi, which takes around 6 hours.
* Visit the famous Kashi Vishwanath temple in Guptkashi.
* Spend the night in Guptkashi.

Day 3: Guptkashi - Kedarnath:

* Start an early trek of 14 km (8.7 miles) to Kedarnath, passing through dense forests and lush meadows. It might take around 7-8 hours to reach the temple.
* Visit the majestic Kedarnath Temple and perform the rituals.
* Enjoy the evening aarti at the temple.

Day 4: Kedarnath:

* Visit the famous Tapt Kund, a natural hot spring, and take a dip to purify your soul.
* Go on a trek to the nearby Gaurikund temple, a short journey offering panoramic views of the valley.
* Spend the night at the base camp or return to Guptkashi.

Day 5: Guptkashi - Deoria Tal:

* Start for Deoria Tal, the famous lake with a serene and peaceful atmosphere. Trek through the dense forests and pass by the river.
* Spend the day at the lake and enjoy the sunset.

Day 6: Deoria Tal - Guptkashi - Rishikesh:

* Board the bus or taxi for Rishikesh from Guptkashi and spend the rest of the day shopping and visiting the sights.

Day 7: Departure:

* Depart from Rishikesh as per your schedule.

**Travel Tips**

* Pack a waterproof backpack to avoid damage due to sudden rain showers.
* Wear layers of warm clothing and waterproof shoes suitable for trekking.
* Bring power banks, water bottles, and a first-aid kit to tackle emergencies.
* Be prepared for changing weather and high altitude, especially if you are prone to altitude sickness.
* Hire a qualified guide for trekking in Kedarnath to ensure your safety.

**Budget Tips**

* Accommodation in Rishikesh and Guptkashi can be quite affordable if booked in advance. 
* Hire a shared taxi or bus for travel to minimize transport costs.
* Opt for budget-friendly options like dal makhani, roti, and sabzi at the local dhabas for food instead of luxury hotels.
* Buy local handicrafts and souvenirs in Guptkashi for a reasonable price.

**Conclusion**

Located amidst the majestic Himalayas, Kedarnath beckons travelers to explore its breathtaking beauty, vibrant culture, and deep spiritual significance. Whether you are a seasoned trekker, a devotee, or a nature enthusiast, this pilgrimage town offers a once-in-a-lifetime experience that touches your heart, mind, and soul.
