
# Houseboat stay Kerala guide

**Discover the Serenity of Kerala: A Guide to a Luxurious Houseboat Stay**

Kerala, a state in the southernmost part of India, is renowned for its lush backwaters, rich cultural heritage, and breathtaking scenic beauty. A houseboat stay in Kerala is a dream vacation for many travelers, offering a unique opportunity to experience the tranquility and magic of the region. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the best time to visit, how to reach, top places to visit, and a day-wise itinerary, along with valuable travel and budget tips.

**Introduction**

Located in the tropical region of India, Kerala is often referred to as the 'God's Own Country.' The state's stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality make it an ideal destination for a relaxing and rejuvenating vacation. A houseboat stay in Kerala is a fantastic way to experience the tranquility of the backwaters, enjoy the local culture, and engage in various recreational activities. Whether you are looking for a romantic getaway or a fun-filled family holiday, a houseboat stay in Kerala has something for everyone.

**Best Time to Visit**

The best time to visit Kerala is from September to May, when the weather is pleasant and ideal for a houseboat stay. The summer months (June to August) can be hot and humid, while the monsoon season (June to November) may cause disruptions in the backwater navigation. Therefore, it's essential to plan your trip during the best season to make the most of your houseboat stay.

**How to Reach**

Kerala is well-connected to major cities in India and abroad. The state has two international airports, Cochin International Airport (COK) and Thiruvananthapuram International Airport (TRV). You can also reach Kerala by train or bus from various parts of the country. The nearest railway station to Alleppey, a popular hub for houseboat stays, is Alleppey Railway Station, which is connected to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Cochin.

**Top Places to Visit**

During your houseboat stay in Kerala, you will have the opportunity to visit various places and experience the local culture. Here are some top places to visit:

1. **Alleppey**: Alleppey, also known as Alappuzha, is a charming town on the banks of the Vembanad Lake. It's a popular hub for houseboat stays and offers stunning views of the backwaters.
2. **Kumarakom**: Located near Alleppey, Kumarakom is a picturesque village surrounded by lush greens and waterways. It's an ideal spot for birdwatching and enjoying the local cuisine.
3. **Marari Beach**: Marari Beach, located between Alleppey and Cherthala, is a scenic beach with crystal-clear waters and golden sand. It's perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.
4. **Backwaters of Kerala**: The backwaters of Kerala are a unique and thrilling experience. You can take a boat ride through the tranquil waters and get a glimpse of the local life.
5. **Kerala Backwater Cruises**: A Kerala backwater cruise is a must-do experience during your houseboat stay. You can enjoy the scenic beauty of the backwaters, engage in local fishing, and experience the cultural heritage of Kerala.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Here's a sample day-wise itinerary for your houseboat stay in Kerala:

**Day 1: Arrival and Check-in**

* Arrive at Alleppey Railway Station
* Check-in to your houseboat
* Relax and unwind on the deck
* Enjoy a traditional Kerala lunch
* Explore the nearby villages and local markets in the evening

**Day 2: Backwater Cruise and Birdwatching**

* Take a backwater cruise through the tranquil waters
* Engage in birdwatching and spot various species of birds
* Enjoy a picnic lunch on a floating island
* Visit the nearby Marari Beach in the evening

**Day 3: Local Exploration and Fishing**

* Explore the nearby villages and local markets
* Engage in local fishing and experience the thrill of catching freshwater fish
* Enjoy a traditional Kerala dinner on board
* Watch a traditional Kalaripayattu performance in the evening

**Day 4: Departure**

* Check-out from your houseboat
* Visit the nearby Alleppey Fort
* Relax on the beach or engage in water sports before departing

**Travel Tips**

To make the most of your houseboat stay in Kerala, here are some valuable travel tips:

1. **Book your houseboat in advance**: Kerala is a popular destination, and it's essential to book your houseboat in advance to avoid any last-minute hassles.
2. **Choose a licensed houseboat operator**: Ensure that your houseboat operator is licensed and has a good reputation in the industry.
3. **Pack light and comfortable**: Pack light and comfortable clothing, as you will be spending most of your time on the houseboat.
4. **Bring sunscreen and insect repellent**: Don't forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent to protect yourself from the sun and mosquitoes.

**Budget Tips**

To enjoy your houseboat stay in Kerala without burning a hole in your pocket, here are some budget tips:

1. **Book a budget-friendly houseboat**: Look for budget-friendly houseboat options, which offer affordable rates without compromising on quality.
2. **Plan your itinerary in advance**: Plan your itinerary in advance to avoid any last-minute expenses.
3. **Cook your own meals**: If possible, cook your own meals on board to save on food expenses.
4. **Engage in local activities**: Engage in local activities, such as birdwatching and fishing, to experience the local culture without incurring any additional costs.

**Conclusion**

In conclusion, a houseboat stay in Kerala is a magical experience that combines natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. With this comprehensive guide, you are all set to explore the serenity of Kerala and create lifelong memories. Whether you're looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventure-filled holiday, Kerala has something for everyone. So why wait? Book your houseboat stay in Kerala today and experience the magic of this wonderful destination!
