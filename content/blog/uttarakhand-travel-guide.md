
# Uttarakhand travel guide

**Discover the Breathtaking Beauty of Uttarakhand: A Comprehensive Travel Guide**

Nestled in the northern part of India, Uttarakhand is a state that boasts breathtaking natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a plethora of adventure activities. Known as the 'Land of the Gods,' Uttarakhand is home to the majestic Himalayas, serene valleys, and picturesque hill stations. In this in-depth travel guide, we will explore the best time to visit, how to reach, top places to visit, day-wise itinerary, travel tips, and budget tips to help you plan an unforgettable trip to Uttarakhand.

**Best Time to Visit Uttarakhand**

The best time to visit Uttarakhand depends on your preferences and the activities you have planned. Generally, the state has a temperate climate, with four distinct seasons:

* **Summer (April to June)**: This is the best time to visit Uttarakhand for trekking, hiking, and exploring the scenic valleys. The weather is pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C.
* **Monsoon (July to September)**: This is the best time for rafting, kayaking, and other water sports. The monsoon rains bring life to the parched landscapes, and the scenery becomes even more breathtaking.
* **Winter (October to March)**: This is the best time for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports. The temperatures drop, and the snow-covered peaks offer breathtaking views.

**How to Reach Uttarakhand**

Uttarakhand has several domestic and international airports, including:

* **Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL)**: The nearest international airport is located in Delhi, which is well-connected to major cities worldwide.
* **Jolly Grant Airport (DED)**: This airport in Dehradun is the primary domestic airport in Uttarakhand, with flights connecting to major cities across India.
* **Udhampur Airport (UHP)**: Located in Udhampur, this airport offers flights to Chandigarh and Delhi.

Uttarakhand is also connected by an extensive network of buses and trains. The state has several major railway stations, including:

* **Dehradun Railway Station (DDN)**
* **Haridwar Railway Station (HW)**
* **Roorkee Railway Station (RK)**
* **Rishikesh Railway Station (RKM)**

**Top Places to Visit in Uttarakhand**

Uttarakhand is home to numerous tourist destinations, including:

* **Mussoorie**: Known as the 'Queen of Hills,' Mussoorie is a picturesque hill station with breathtaking views of the Himalayas.
* **Rishikesh**: This holy city is a popular destination for adventure sports, including rafting, kayaking, and bungee jumping.
* **Haridwar**: Considered one of the seven sacred cities in Hinduism, Haridwar is a spiritual hub with a rich cultural heritage.
* **Nainital**: This scenic hill station is famous for its lakes, forests, and breathtaking mountain views.
* **Kedarnath**: This ancient temple town is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas.
* **Auli**: This picturesque ski resort is located in the Garhwal Himalayas and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
* **Lansdowne**: This charming hill station is surrounded by forests and offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas.

**Day-wise Itinerary for Uttarakhand**

Here is a sample day-wise itinerary for a 7-day trip to Uttarakhand:

Day 1: Arrival in Dehradun and drive to Mussoorie

* Morning: Arrive in Dehradun and drive to Mussoorie ( approx. 35 km, 1-hour drive)
* Afternoon: Explore the mall road, visit the Kempty Falls, and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Himalayas
* Evening: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant and rest at the hotel

Day 2: Mussoorie

* Morning: Visit the Gun Hill ( approx. 20 km, 45-minute drive), the highest point in Mussoorie
* Afternoon: Explore the Cloud's End and the Lal Tibba, and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Himalayas
* Evening: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant and rest at the hotel

Day 3: Mussoorie to Rishikesh

* Morning: Drive to Rishikesh ( approx. 60 km, 2-hour drive)
* Afternoon: Explore the Rishikesh city, visit the Lakshman Temple, and enjoy the scenic views of the Ganges River
* Evening: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant and rest at the hotel

Day 4: Rishikesh

* Morning: Visit the Rishikesh White Water Rafting Center and enjoy a thrilling rafting experience
* Afternoon: Explore the Triveni Ghat, the Shivananda Ashram, and the Geeta Bhawan
* Evening: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant and rest at the hotel

Day 5: Rishikesh to Haridwar

* Morning: Drive to Haridwar ( approx. 30 km, 1-hour drive)
* Afternoon: Explore the Haridwar city, visit the Har Ki Pauri Ghat, and enjoy the scenic views of the Ganges River
* Evening: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant and rest at the hotel

Day 6: Haridwar to Auli

* Morning: Drive to Auli ( approx. 280 km, 7-hour drive)
* Afternoon: Explore the Auli resort and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Himalayas
* Evening: Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant and rest at the hotel

Day 7: Auli

* Morning: Enjoy skiing or snowboarding at the Auli resort
* Afternoon: Explore the Joshimath town and enjoy the scenic views of the Himalayas
* Evening: Depart from Auli and head to Dehradun

**Travel Tips for Uttarakhand**

Here are some essential travel tips for a comfortable and enjoyable trip to Uttarakhand:

* **Weather**: Check the weather forecast before your trip and pack accordingly.
* **Clothing**: Wear comfortable clothing and layers for colder weather.
* **Transportation**: Hire a car or taxi for local transportation.
* **Accommodation**: Book your hotel or resort in advance to avoid last-minute hassles.
* **Food**: Try local cuisine and drinks, such as the Garhwali Thali and the Chammagadda.
* **Safety**: Carry a first-aid kit, stay hydrated, and follow local guidelines.

**Budget Tips for Uttarakhand**

Here are some budget tips to help you save money on your trip to Uttarakhand:

* **Accommodation**: Stay in budget-friendly hotels or resorts.
* **Food**: Eat at local restaurants or street food stalls.
* **Transportation**: Hire a car or taxi for local transportation.
* **Activities**: Choose free or low-cost activities, such as trekking or visiting the local markets.
* **Tour operators**: Choose local tour operators who offer affordable packages.

Don't forget to try local cuisine and drinks, and follow local guidelines for a safe and enjoyable trip.

Whether you are a nature lover, an adventure enthusiast, or a spiritual seeker, Uttarakhand has something for everyone. So pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready to explore the breathtaking beauty of this paradise on earth.
