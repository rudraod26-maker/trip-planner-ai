
# 3 day Varanasi itinerary

**Unveiling the Ancient Charm of Varanasi: A 3-Day Itinerary**

Tucked away in the eastern part of Uttar Pradesh, India, Varanasi is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. A city that embodies the essence of Hinduism, Varanasi is a destination that will leave you spellbound and inspired. This 3,000-year-old city is home to the iconic Ganges River, ancient temples, vibrant ghats, and bustling streets that are a treat for the senses. In this travel guide, we'll take you on a journey through the best of Varanasi, highlighting the top places to visit, travel tips, and budget-friendly suggestions to make your trip unforgettable.

**Best Time to Visit Varanasi**

Varanasi is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the winter months of November to March. The weather is cooler and more pleasant, making it ideal for exploring the city's ancient streets and temples. Summer can be scorching hot, while monsoon brings with it heavy rainfall, making it challenging to navigate the city.

**How to Reach Varanasi**

Varanasi International Airport (VNS) is the primary airport in the city, receiving flights from major Indian cities and international destinations. You can also reach Varanasi by train, with the Varanasi Junction (BHB) railway station being well-connected to major cities across India. Alternatively, take a bus or taxi from nearby cities like Lucknow, Kanpur, or Allahabad.

**Top Places to Visit**

Varanasi is a treasure trove of historical landmarks, spiritual sites, and cultural attractions. Here are some of the top places to visit in Varanasi:

1. **Dashashwamedh Ghat**: The most iconic ghat in Varanasi, Dashashwamedh is a sacred site where pilgrims come to worship Lord Shiva. It's the point of origin for the evening aarti (prayer ceremony) that takes place every day.
2. **Kashi Vishwanath Temple**: One of the most revered Hindu temples, Kashi Vishwanath is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is said to be one of the twelve Jyotirlingas (lingams of light). The temple complex is a riot of colors, with intricate carvings, and a stunning golden spire piercing the sky.
3. **Man Mandir Ghat**: A lesser-known ghat, Man Mandir is a peaceful oasis amidst the chaos of the city. Take a boat ride here to witness the serene beauty of the Ganges.
4. **Ramnagar Fort**: Located on the other side of the Ganges, Ramnagar Fort is a grand structure that houses the Narada Ghat, a beautiful temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu.
5. **Bharat Mata Temple**: A unique temple dedicated to Mother India, Bharat Mata is a must-visit for its stunning murals depicting India's rich cultural heritage.
6. **Sarnath Museum**: A short drive away from Varanasi, Sarnath Museum is home to some of the most significant artifacts related to Buddhism, including the original Ashoka Pillar.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

To experience the best of Varanasi, follow this day-wise itinerary:

### Day 1

* Morning: Start with a boat ride on the Ganges from Dashashwamedh Ghat.
* Visit the Kashi Vishwanath Temple and witness the evening aarti (prayer ceremony).
* Explore the bustling lanes of Varanasi, taking in the sights, sounds, and aromas of the city.

### Day 2

* Morning: Visit the Man Mandir Ghat and take a boat ride to witness the serene beauty of the Ganges.
* Head to Ramnagar Fort, where you can visit the Narada Ghat and explore the fort's impressive structure.
* Visit the Bharat Mata Temple in the evening, taking in the stunning murals depicting India's rich cultural heritage.

### Day 3

* Visit the Sarnath Museum to learn about Indian history and its connections to Buddhism.
* Explore the Sarnath Archaeological Site, where Lord Buddha first delivered his teachings after enlightenment.
* Return to Varanasi, with time to relax and enjoy the city's vibrant atmosphere.

**Travel Tips**

To make your trip to Varanasi a smooth and enjoyable experience, follow these travel tips:

1. **Dress modestly**: Varanasi is a spiritual city, and you're expected to dress modestly. Avoid revealing clothing and cover your shoulders and knees as a sign of respect.
2. **Respect local customs**: Avoid taking photos or videos at temples or during aarti ceremonies without permission. Be mindful of your surroundings and respect local customs.
3. **Stay hydrated**: Drinking plenty of water is essential in Varanasi's hot and humid climate. Bring a refillable water bottle with you to avoid buying plastic bottles.
4. **Eat local cuisine**: Indulge in the rich flavors of Varanasi's street food, from kachoris to pani puris. Be adventurous, but also ensure that you eat at reputable stalls.
5. **Haggling is part of the experience**: Be prepared to haggle at markets and street stalls. The art of haggling is a part of the Varanasi experience.

**Budget Tips**

To travel to Varanasi on a budget, follow these tips:

1. **Accommodation**: Stay at budget-friendly hotels or guesthouses, such as the Ganges Guest House or the Hotel Gangey.
2. **Transportation**: Use local transportation, such as autorickshaws, to get around the city.
3. **Food**: Eat at local street stalls or try affordable Indian restaurants.
4. **Attractions**: Visit major attractions during the day to avoid the evening crowds and higher prices.
5. **Barter**: Haggling is an art in Varanasi, so don't be afraid to negotiate prices.

**Conclusion**

Varanasi is a city that will leave you spellbound and inspired. With its rich history, spiritual significance, and vibrant culture, it's an experience that will stay with you forever. By following this 3-day itinerary, travel tips, and budget suggestions, you'll be well-prepared to unravel the mysteries of this incredible city. Remember to respect local customs, stay hydrated, and indulge in the local cuisine. For a truly unforgettable experience, add Varanasi to your travels list today.
