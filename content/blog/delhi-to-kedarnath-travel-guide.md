**Delhi to Kedarnath Travel Guide: A Spiritual Journey to the Foot of the Himalayas**

Introduction:

Located in the Garhwal Himalayas, Kedarnath is a sacred Hindu pilgrimage site and one of the most revered temples in India. Perched at an altitude of 3,553 meters, this temple town offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The journey from Delhi to Kedarnath is a challenging but rewarding experience, perfect for those seeking spiritual enlightenment and adventure. In this comprehensive travel guide, we'll take you through the best time to visit, top places to explore, a day-wise itinerary, travel tips, and budget tips to help you plan an unforgettable trip to Kedarnath.

**Best Time to Visit:**

The best time to visit Kedarnath is from April to June and September to November, when the temperatures are mild and the snow has melted. However, if you're planning to trek to the temple, the best time is from May to June, when the snow has melted and the trails are accessible.

**Top Places to Visit:**

1. **Kedarnath Temple**: The temple is a must-visit destination and a major pilgrimage site for Hindus. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the temple is adorned with intricate carvings and statues of various deities.
2. **Bhim Pul**: A picturesque bridge built by Bhima, one of the Pandava brothers, is a popular spot for photography.
3. **Tapti Kali**: Located at the base of the hill, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Kali and is a must-visit for those seeking spiritual enlightenment.
4. **Kedarnath Village**: The village is a collection of wooden houses and is home to the locals who offer warm hospitality and delicious food.
5. **Buddha Ropeway**: A scenic cable car ride that connects Gaurikund to Kedarnath offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

**Day-wise Itinerary:**

Day 1: Delhi to Haridwar (250 km, 5 hours)

* Depart from Delhi by train or bus
* Arrive at Haridwar and check into a hotel
* Visit Har Ki Paudi, a holy bathing ghat, and take a stroll along the Ganges River

Day 2: Haridwar to Rishikesh (22 km, 1 hour)

* Take a bus or taxi to Rishikesh
* Visit Lakshman Jhula, a 100-year-old iron bridge, and explore the shops and cafes
* Visit Tera Manzil Temple, a beautiful temple with intricate carvings

Day 3: Rishikesh to Guptkashi (200 km, 6 hours)

* Depart from Rishikesh by bus or taxi
* Visit the town of Guptkashi, with its beautiful temple and scenic views
* Explore the local market and try some local cuisine

Day 4: Guptkashi to Kedarnath (20 km, 1.5 hours)

* Take a bus or taxi to Gaurikund
* Trek to Kedarnath (14 km, 5-6 hours)
* Visit the Kedarnath Temple and explore the surrounding area

Day 5: Kedarnath to Guptkashi (20 km, 1.5 hours)

* Take a bus or taxi back to Gaurikund
* Visit the ancient temple of Gauri Kund and take a bath in the hot springs
* Return to Guptkashi for the night

Day 6: Guptkashi to Rishikesh (200 km, 6 hours)

* Take a bus or taxi to Rishikesh
* Visit the town and explore the local market
* Depart from Rishikesh for Delhi

**Travel Tips:**

1. **Acclimatize**: Kedarnath is located at a high altitude, so it's essential to acclimatize before trekking to the temple.
2. **Physical Condition**: The trek to Kedarnath is challenging, so it's essential to have a good physical condition.
3. **Weather**: The weather in Kedarnath is unpredictable, so it's essential to pack layers and carry a raincoat.
4. **Food**: Try local cuisine, such as thali and kheer, which is delicious and affordable.
5. **Accommodation**: Book a hotel or guesthouse in advance to avoid last-minute hassles.

**Budget Tips:**

1. **Accommodation**: Stay in budget hotels or guesthouses, which are available at affordable rates.
2. **Food**: Eat at local restaurants or dhabas, which offer delicious food at affordable prices.
3. **Transportation**: Take public transport or walk between towns to save money.
4. **Trekking**: Hire a local guide or trek solo to save money.
5. **Attractions**: Visit the temple and other attractions at a budget-friendly rate.

With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and spiritual significance, Kedarnath is a must-visit destination for those seeking adventure and enlightenment. Follow the day-wise itinerary, travel tips, and budget tips to make the most of your trip and create unforgettable memories.
