
# Delhi 3 day itinerary

**Discover the Charm of Delhi: A 3-Day Itinerary**

Are you ready to immerse yourself in the vibrant culture, rich history, and mouth-watering cuisine of India's capital city? Look no further than Delhi, a metropolis that seamlessly blends ancient and modern traditions. In this travel blog, we'll guide you through the best of Delhi, showcasing the top places to visit, must-try experiences, and valuable travel tips to make the most of your 3-day adventure.

**Best Time to Visit Delhi**

The best time to visit Delhi is from October to March, when the weather is pleasant and the temperature ranges from 10°C to 25°C (50°F to 77°F). Avoid visiting during the hot summer months (April to June) or the chilly winters (January to February), when the mercury soars or dips.

**How to Reach Delhi**

Delhi is well-connected to major cities in India and the world via air, rail, and road. Here are the options:

- **By Air:** The capital city has three international airports: Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL), Delhi Cantonment Airport, and Hindon Airport. Regular flights operate from major international hubs to Delhi.
- **By Rail:** Delhi is a major railway junction, with three main railway stations: New Delhi Railway Station, Old Delhi Railway Station, and Bijwasan Railway Station. You can reach Delhi by train from most Indian cities.
- **By Road:** Delhi is connected to neighboring cities and states via a network of national and state highways. Regular bus services operate from major bus stations in the city.

**Top Places to Visit in Delhi**

Delhi is a treasure trove of historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and mouth-watering food scenes. Here are the top places to visit in Delhi:

1.  **Red Fort (Lal Qila):** A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Red Fort is a magnificent fort built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century.
2.  **Qutub Minar:** This 73-meter (240 feet) tall monument is one of the tallest minarets in India, built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak in 1199.
3.  **India Gate:** A war memorial built in the memory of Indian soldiers who died in World War I, India Gate is a symbol of national pride.
4.  **Akshardham Temple:** A beautiful Hindu temple complex built in the 21st century, Akshardham is a showcase of Indian culture and spirituality.
5.  **Hauz Khas Village:** A charming village in the heart of the city, Hauz Khas is famous for its ancient architecture, boutique shops, and trendy restaurants.
6.  **Chandni Chowk:** A bustling marketplace in the walled city, Chandni Chowk is a sensory experience, with colorful street food, street performers, and historic landmarks.
7.  **National Museum:** A premier cultural institution, the National Museum showcases India's rich artistic, cultural, and historical heritage.

**Day-wise Itinerary for Delhi**

To make the most of your 3-day trip to Delhi, follow this day-wise itinerary:

**Day 1: Historical Landmarks**

- **Morning:** Visit Red Fort (Lal Qila) and explore its grandeur and beauty.
- **Afternoon:** Head to India Gate and Qutub Minar, two iconic landmarks that symbolize India's rich history and culture.
- **Evening:** Explore the vibrant street life of Chandni Chowk and try some delicious street food.

**Day 2: Cultural Attractions**

- **Morning:** Visit the National Museum to explore India's rich cultural heritage.
- **Afternoon:** Head to Akshardham Temple, a beautiful Hindu temple complex that showcases Indian culture and spirituality.
- **Evening:** Relax in the lush green gardens of Hauz Khas Village and enjoy a peaceful evening.

**Day 3: Exploring Modern Delhi**

- **Morning:** Visit the bustling markets of Sarojini Nagar and try some local shopping.
- **Afternoon:** Head to the trendy neighborhood of Hauz Khas and explore its boutique shops, art galleries, and cafes.
- **Evening:** Enjoy a sunset view of the city from the rooftop of a cafe or restaurant in Hauz Khas.

**Travel Tips for Delhi**

- **Safety:** Be mindful of your belongings and avoid traveling alone at night. Avoid using unlicensed taxis or autorickshaws.
- **Dress Code:** Dress modestly when visiting temples, mosques, or other places of worship.
- **Language:** Hindi and English are the official languages of Delhi. Learn a few basic Hindi phrases to interact with locals.
- **Currency:** The Indian rupee (INR) is the local currency. You can exchange currency at airports, banks, or currency exchange booths.

**Budget Tips for Delhi**

- **Accommodation:** Stay in a budget-friendly hotel or hostel, which costs between ₹500-₹2,000 (USD 7-28) per night.
- **Food:** Eat at street stalls, food trucks, or small restaurants, which cost between ₹100-₹300 (USD 1.50-4.50) per meal.
- **Transportation:** Use public transport, such as metro trains or buses, which costs between ₹10-₹50 (USD 0.15-0.80) per ride.

**Conclusion**

Delhi is a city that will leave you in awe, with its rich history, vibrant culture, and mouth-watering food scene. With this 3-day itinerary, you'll be able to explore the best of Delhi, from historical landmarks to cultural attractions and trendy neighborhoods. Remember to stay safe, dress modestly, and learn a few basic Hindi phrases to interact with locals. With a budget-friendly approach, you can enjoy the best of Delhi without breaking the bank.
