
# Jaipur 2 day itinerary

**Unlock the Vibrant Spirit of Jaipur: A 2-Day Itinerary**

Jaipur, the Pink City of India, is a treasure trove of rich history, breathtaking architecture, and warm hospitality. This 2-day itinerary will guide you through the most iconic attractions, hidden gems, and insider tips to make your trip an unforgettable experience.

**Introduction**

Located in the state of Rajasthan, Jaipur is the capital city of the same name. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Jaipur is a testament to the region's architectural excellence, weaving together the traditions of Islamic, Gujarati, and Rajasthani styles. As you step into this vibrant city, you're transported to a world of opulence, grandeur, and beauty, making it an ideal destination for history buffs, foodies, and shopaholics alike.

**Best Time to Visit**

Jaipur enjoys a semi-arid climate, characterized by extreme temperatures during summer (April-May) and cold winters (December-January). The best time to visit Jaipur is between October and February, when the weather is mild and pleasant, with an average temperature ranging from 18°C to 25°C.

**How to Reach**

Jaipur is well-connected to major cities in India and abroad, making it easy to reach by air, rail, or road.

By Air:
Jaipur International Airport (JAI) is situated 13 km from the city center and receives flights from major domestic and international airlines, including Mumbai, Delhi, and London.

By Rail:
Jaipur Junction is a major railway station in the city, connected to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata. The Indian Railways offers multiple trains per day, including the luxurious Palace on Wheels.

By Road:
Jaipur is located on the golden quadrilateral of the National Highway, making it easily accessible by road. Taxis, buses, and private cabs are readily available from the city bus stand or the Jaipur airport.

**Top Places to Visit**

Jaipur's stunning architecture, rich history, and colorful markets will captivate your senses and make it a memorable experience. Here are the top places to visit in Jaipur:

1. **Hawa Mahal**: The iconic Hawa Mahal, or Palace of Winds, is a stunning example of Rajasthani architecture, built by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh in 1799. Its labyrinthine passageways, delicate filigree work, and intricate windows are a must-see.
2. **City Palace**: The City Palace complex houses several stunning buildings, including the Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, and Chandra Mahal. Be sure to explore the palace's sprawling courtyards, gardens, and the iconic Mubarak Mahal.
3. **Jantar Mantar**: This ancient observatory, built in 1734, is an astronomical marvel, featuring an array of astronomical instruments and tools created by Maharaja Jai Singh II.
4. **Amber Fort**: Perched atop a hill, the iconic Amber Fort is a testament to Rajasthani architecture, built by Maharaja Mansingh in 1592. Don't miss the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
5. **Chokhi Dhani**: Step into this traditional Rajasthani village, complete with intricately designed huts, colorful markets, and local artisans displaying their wares.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Here's a suggested 2-day itinerary to make the most of your trip to Jaipur:

**Day 1:**

1. Visit the Hawa Mahal in the morning, followed by breakfast at a nearby café.
2. Explore the City Palace complex, including the Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, and Chandra Mahal.
3. Visit the Jantar Mantar and marvel at its ancient astronomical instruments.
4. Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant serving traditional Rajasthani cuisine.
5. Visit the Amber Fort in the evening, and watch the stunning sunset over the Aravali hills.

**Day 2:**

1. Visit the famous Chokhi Dhani in the morning, and immerse yourself in traditional Rajasthani culture.
2. Explore the vibrant bazaars, including the Johari Bazaar and Tripolia Bazaar, for some excellent shopping and snack opportunities.
3. Visit the Anokhi Museum, dedicated to the art of block printing, and learn about the traditional techniques used by local artisans.
4. Visit the Nahargarh Fort, perched atop Aravali hills, for stunning views of the city.

**Travel Tips**

Jaipur is a city of vibrant energy, warm hospitality, and rich culture. Here are some insider tips to make the most of your trip:

* Book your accommodations in advance, as Jaipur is a popular tourist destination.
* Be prepared for crowds, especially during peak season (October to February).
* Hire a guide or local driver to navigate the city, as Jaipur has a complex network of streets and alleys.
* Don't miss the iconic street food, such as kachoris, chaat, and snacks like pyaaz kachoris.
* Be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting temples and palaces.

**Budget Tips**

Jaipur is a relatively affordable destination, with plenty of options to suit all budgets. Here are some budget tips to help you save:

* Accommodation: Stay in budget-friendly guesthouses or hostels, starting from ₹500-₹1,000 per night.
* Food: Try street food, local snacks, and eat at budget-friendly restaurants, starting from ₹50-₹100 per meal.
* Transportation: Hire an auto-rickshaw or taxi for ₹500-₹1,000 per day, or take the public bus for ₹10-₹50.
* Attractions: Buy a combo ticket for major attractions, starting from ₹500-₹1,000 per person.

**Conclusion**

Jaipur, the Pink City of India, is a treasure trove of rich history, stunning architecture, and warm hospitality. Whether you're a history buff, a foodie, or a shopaholic, Jaipur has something for everyone. With this 2-day itinerary, insider tips, and budget advice, you're ready to unlock the vibrant spirit of Jaipur and make it an unforgettable experience.
