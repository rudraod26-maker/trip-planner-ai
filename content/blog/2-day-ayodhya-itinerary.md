
# 2 day Ayodhya itinerary

**Discover the Holy City of Ayodhya: A 2-Day Itinerary**

Ayodhya, a sacred city in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh, is steeped in history and spiritual significance. Also known as the birthplace of Lord Rama, this city is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to experience the rich cultural heritage of India. In this blog, we'll guide you through the best time to visit, how to reach Ayodhya, top places to visit, a suggested day-wise itinerary, travel tips, and budget tips to make the most of your 2-day adventure.

**Introduction**

Located in the Faizabad district, Ayodhya is approximately 6 hours away from Lucknow, the capital city of Uttar Pradesh. This ancient city is a pilgrimage site for millions of Hindus worldwide, who come to pay their respects to Lord Rama, the seventh incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Ayodhya is a city of numerous temples, monuments, and historical sites, offering travelers a glimpse into India's rich cultural and spiritual heritage.

**Best Time to Visit**

The best time to visit Ayodhya is during the winter months, from October to February, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be quite hot, while monsoon season can be unpredictable. If you're planning to visit during the peak tourist season, be prepared for large crowds and higher prices for accommodations and transportation.

**How to Reach**

Ayodhya is well-connected by road, rail, and air. Here are the best ways to reach Ayodhya:

* **By Air:** The nearest airport is the Lucknow Chaudhary Charan Singh Airport (LKO), which is approximately 130 km away from Ayodhya. You can take a taxi, bus, or hire a private vehicle to reach Ayodhya from the airport.
* **By Train:** Ayodhya has its own railway station, which is well-connected to major cities in India. You can take a train from Delhi, Kolkata, or Lucknow to reach Ayodhya.
* **By Road:** Ayodhya is connected to major cities by a network of highways. You can take a bus or hire a private vehicle to reach Ayodhya from Lucknow or other nearby cities.

**Top Places to Visit**

Ayodhya is home to numerous temples, monuments, and historical sites, each with its own unique significance and charm. Here are some of the top places to visit in Ayodhya:

* **Ram Janmabhoomi:** This sacred site is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama and attracts millions of devotees every year.
* **Hanumangarhi:** This beautiful temple is dedicated to Lord Hanuman and offers stunning views of the city.
* **Sri Govind Dev Ji Temple:** This temple is one of the most important shrines in Ayodhya and is dedicated to Lord Govind.
* **Nageshwarnath Temple:** This ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the oldest temples in Ayodhya.
* **Ayodhya Museum:** This museum showcases the history and cultural heritage of Ayodhya and is a must-visit for history buffs.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Here's a suggested 2-day itinerary for Ayodhya:

**Day 1**

* 8:00 am: Start your day with a visit to the Ram Janmabhoomi site, which is a sacred site for millions of Hindus.
* 10:00 am: Visit the Hanumangarhi temple, which offers stunning views of the city.
* 12:00 pm: Take a break for lunch at a nearby restaurant or try some local street food.
* 2:00 pm: Visit the Sri Govind Dev Ji Temple, which is one of the most important shrines in Ayodhya.
* 4:00 pm: Explore the local markets and try some local handicrafts and souvenirs.
* 6:00 pm: Enjoy a peaceful evening at the Nageshwarnath Temple, which is a beautiful and serene site.

**Day 2**

* 8:00 am: Start your day with a visit to the Ayodhya Museum, which showcases the history and cultural heritage of Ayodhya.
* 10:00 am: Visit the Kuberakund and the nearby Ramkot, which offer stunning views of the city.
* 12:00 pm: Take a break for lunch at a nearby restaurant or try some local street food.
* 2:00 pm: Visit the Raghav Ballabh Temple, which is a beautiful temple dedicated to Lord Rama.
* 4:00 pm: Explore the local markets and try some local handicrafts and souvenirs.
* 6:00 pm: End your day with a peaceful evening at the Hanumangarhi temple.

**Travel Tips**

Here are some travel tips to make the most of your trip to Ayodhya:

* Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting sacred sites.
* Wear comfortable clothing and shoes, as you'll be doing a lot of walking.
* Bring sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun.
* Try local food and snacks, which are delicious and nutritious.
* Be prepared for large crowds and long queues, especially during peak tourist season.

**Budget Tips**

Here are some budget tips to make your trip to Ayodhya affordable:

* Accommodation: Stay at a budget-friendly guesthouse or hotel, which costs around ₹500-₹1000 per night.
* Food: Try local street food or eat at a nearby restaurant, which costs around ₹100-₹200 per meal.
* Transportation: Take a bus or taxi from the airport or railway station, which costs around ₹200-₹500.
* Entry fees: Pay for entry fees at the temples and sites, which costs around ₹10-₹50 per person.

**Conclusion**

Ayodhya is a holy city that offers travelers a glimpse into India's rich cultural and spiritual heritage. With its numerous temples, monuments, and historical sites, Ayodhya is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, culture, and spirituality. This 2-day itinerary provides a comprehensive guide to exploring the best of Ayodhya, and with these travel and budget tips, you'll be able to make the most of your trip.
