
# 3 day Ujjain itinerary

**Discover the Sacred City of Ujjain: A 3-Day Itinerary**

Located on the western banks of the Shipra River in Madhya Pradesh, India, Ujjain is one of the seven sacred cities in Hinduism and a prominent pilgrimage site. The city boasts a rich history, cultural heritage, and breathtaking architecture, making it a must-visit destination for travelers. In this blog, we will take you through the best time to visit Ujjain, how to reach this sacred city, top places to visit, a day-wise itinerary, and valuable travel tips to make the most of your trip.

**Best Time to Visit Ujjain**

The best time to visit Ujjain is during the winter season, from October to February, when the weather is mild and pleasant, making it ideal for sightseeing. Summer months (March to May) can be hot and humid, while monsoon (June to September) brings heavy rainfall, making it difficult to plan outdoor activities.

**How to Reach Ujjain**

Reaching Ujjain is relatively easy, with several transportation options available:

1. **By Air**: The nearest airport is the Devi Ahilyabai Holkar Airport in Indore (55 km from Ujjain). From here, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Ujjain.
2. **By Train**: Ujjain has a railway station, which connects it to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata. Trains like the Madhya Pradesh Sampark Kranti Express (22108) and the Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Katra - Indore Superfast Express (12920) pass through Ujjain.
3. **By Road**: Ujjain is well-connected to major cities through National Highways. You can hire a taxi or bus from nearby cities like Indore (45 km), Bhopal (190 km), or Gwalior (180 km).

**Top Places to Visit**

Ujjain is a treasure trove of historical and cultural monuments, making it a paradise for travelers. Here are the top places to visit:

1. **Mahakaleshwar Temple**: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this sacred temple is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas in India.
2. **Shri Kumbh Mela Ground**: Witness the largest human gathering in the world at the confluence of the Shipra River and the Ganges.
3. **Dewas Fort**: This 200-year-old fort is an excellent example of Rajput architecture and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
4. **Ramghat**: Boating on the Shipra River is a popular activity in Ujjain, and Ramghat is the perfect spot to do so.
5. **Kamadgiri Hill**: Located on the banks of the Shipra River, this hill is a must-visit for its panoramic views of the city.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

To make the most of your 3-day trip to Ujjain, here's a suggested day-wise itinerary:

**Day 1:**

* Morning: Visit the Mahakaleshwar Temple and take a dip in the nearby Gomti Kund.
* Afternoon: Explore the city's vibrant markets and try some local cuisine, like Jalebi and Rabri.
* Evening: Watch the sunset from the Dewas Fort.

**Day 2:**

* Morning: Visit the Shri Kumbh Mela Ground and witness the vibrant atmosphere, but avoid peak Kumbh years.
* Afternoon: Take a boat ride on the Shipra River from Ramghat and enjoy the scenic views.
* Evening: Try some authentic local cuisine at one of the many restaurants in the city.

**Day 3:**

* Morning: Visit the Kamadgiri Hill and enjoy the panoramic views of the city.
* Afternoon: Spend some time shopping for local handicrafts and souvenirs.
* Evening: Depart for your next destination.

**Travel Tips**

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Ujjain, follow these valuable travel tips:

1. **Respect local customs**: Ujjain is a sacred city, so dress modestly and respect local customs.
2. **Hydrate**: Drink plenty of water and avoid consuming tap water.
3. **Language**: Learn basic Hindi phrases, like 'Namaste' and 'Dhanyavad.'
4. **Safety**: Avoid walking alone at night and stick to reputable taxi services.
5. **Weather**: Pack light and comfortable clothing for the winter season.

**Budget Tips**

To make your trip to Ujjain affordable, follow these budget-friendly tips:

1. **Accommodation**: Choose budget-friendly hotels or guesthouses near the river.
2. **Food**: Eat at local dhabas and try street food, which is affordable and delicious.
3. **Transportation**: Hire a local taxi for the day or use public transport, like buses.
4. **Sightseeing**: Avoid touristy spots and explore local markets and temples.

**Conclusion**

Ujjain, with its rich history, cultural heritage, and breathtaking architecture, is a must-visit destination for travelers. With this 3-day itinerary, you'll experience the best of this sacred city, from sacred temples to vibrant markets. Follow our valuable travel tips and budget-friendly suggestions to make the most of your trip. So, pack your bags, and get ready to explore the majestic city of Ujjain!
