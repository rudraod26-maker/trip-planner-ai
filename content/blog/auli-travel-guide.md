
# Auli travel guide

**Auli Travel Guide: Unveiling the Hidden Gem of India**

**Introduction**

Perched in the lap of the Himalayas, Auli is a serene and picturesque hill station located in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, India. This offbeat destination is often overshadowed by its more popular counterparts, but it has a lot to offer adventurous travelers, nature lovers, and those seeking tranquility. Auli, which translates to "meadow" in Sanskrit, is a haven of towering pine trees, lush green meadows, and breathtaking vistas of the Himalayan range.

In this travel guide, we will take you through the best time to visit Auli, how to reach this secluded destination, top places to visit, a detailed day-wise itinerary, essential travel tips, budget-friendly advice, and conclude with the perfect blend of adventure and serenity that Auli offers.

**Best Time to Visit Auli**

Auli can be visited throughout the year, but the best time to visit is during the winter season from December to February. The temperature remains cool and crisp, with average temperatures ranging from 10°C to 15°C. During this period, Auli is decked up in a blanket of snow, and the slopes are perfect for skiing and snowboarding. April to June and September to November are the best months for trekking and exploring the natural beauty of Auli.

**How to Reach Auli**

Reaching Auli is relatively easy, and you can follow these steps:

1. **By Air:** The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, which is 270 km from Auli. You can take a taxi or bus from the airport to Rishikesh, and then continue your journey to Auli.
2. **By Train:** The nearest railway station is Haridwar, which is 270 km from Auli. From Haridwar, take a bus or taxi to Rishikesh, and then drive to Auli.
3. **By Road:** You can hire a taxi or drive to Auli from Rishikesh or Joshimath, which is the last stop on the motorable road.

**Top Places to Visit in Auli**

Auli has plenty of attractions to offer, including:

1. **Auli Trek:** The trek to Auli is a must-do for adventure enthusiasts. The 13 km trek is moderately challenging and offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas.
2. **Gurson Bugyal:** This lush green meadow is perfect for trekking and offers stunning views of the Himalayan range.
3. **Auli Lake:** A serene lake surrounded by towering pine trees, Auli Lake offers boating and fishing opportunities.
4. **Trishul and Nanda Devi Viewpoint:** These viewpoints offer panoramic views of the Himalayan range and are perfect for photography.
5. **Hemkund Sahib:** A famous Sikh pilgrimage site, Hemkund Sahib is a must-visit for anyone interested in history and culture.

**Day-wise Itinerary**

Here's a suggested itinerary for 5 days in Auli:

Day 1: Arrival and Acclimatization

* Arrive at Rishikesh and check-in at a hotel
* Explore the town and acclimatize to the altitude
* Visit the famous Laxman Jhula and Ram Jhula bridges

Day 2: Auli Trek

* Take an early morning taxi to Auli and start the trek
* Stop at the Gurson Bugyal meadow for lunch and take in the breathtaking views
* Reach Auli in the evening and spend the night at the Auli Tourist Rest House

Day 3: Auli Lake and Trishul and Nanda Devi Viewpoint

* Spend the day exploring Auli Lake and boating
* Visit the Trishul and Nanda Devi viewpoints for panoramic views of the Himalayan range

Day 4: Hemkund Sahib and Valley of Flowers

* Take an early morning taxi to Hemkund Sahib and explore the site
* Visit the Valley of Flowers on the way back to Auli

Day 5: Departure

* Spend the morning exploring Auli and shopping for local handicrafts
* Depart for Rishikesh and then back home

**Travel Tips**

Here are some essential tips for traveling to Auli:

1. **Weather:** Carry warm clothing, gloves, and scarves during the winter months, and carry sunscreen and hats during the summer months.
2. **Altitude:** Acclimatize slowly to the altitude to avoid altitude sickness.
3. **Safety:** Carry a first-aid kit and take necessary precautions while trekking.
4. **Language:** English is widely spoken in Auli, but learning basic Hindi phrases can help.

**Budget Tips**

Here are some budget-friendly tips for traveling to Auli:

1. **Accommodation:** Stay at budget-friendly hotels and guesthouses in Rishikesh and Auli.
2. **Food:** Try local street food and eat at budget-friendly restaurants.
3. **Transport:** Hire a taxi for the day instead of taking individual taxis.
4. **Activities:** Take part in free activities like hiking and trekking.

**Conclusion**

Auli is a hidden gem in the Himalayas, offering a unique blend of adventure and serenity. From trekking and skiing to exploring the natural beauty of the Himalayan range, Auli has plenty to offer adventurous travelers, nature lovers, and those seeking tranquility. With this travel guide, we have provided you with all the essential information to plan your trip to Auli. So pack your bags, grab your camera, and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in Auli!
